  2 23                           EXPLANATION
  2 24 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  2 25 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  2 26    This bill establishes an Iowa employment and education rides
  2 27 initiative in the department of transportation to provide
  2 28 funds to public transit systems for programs and services that
  2 29 provide employment and education transportation to Iowans.
  2 30    The bill defines "employment and education transportation"
  2 31 as an urban or rural program or service that provides an
  2 32 individual with transportation solely to or from a workplace or
  2 33 adult education venue, including but not limited to a community
  2 34 college, evening or weekend class, apprenticeship program, or
  2 35 job training program. The urban or rural program or service
  3  1 includes but is not limited to expanding or sustaining existing
  3  2 transportation services or service hours, including nights and
  3  3 weekends; coordinating ride share services, including car pool
  3  4 or van pool services; and shuttle services.
  3  5    A "public transit system" is defined under Code section
  3  6 324A.1 as an urban or regional transit system providing
  3  7 transit services accessible to the general public and receiving
  3  8 federal, state, or local tax support.
  3  9    The department shall award funds from the initiative on a
  3 10 competitive grant basis. A grant cannot exceed $150,000. A
  3 11 grant application must contain a commitment from the public
  3 12 transit system of at least a dollar=for=dollar match of
  3 13 the grant funds awarded. Grant funds can only be used for
  3 14 operational costs directly associated with providing employment
  3 15 and education transportation and cannot be used for capital
  3 16 expenditures or construction.
  3 17    The bill permits a public transit system to coordinate
  3 18 with other local, state, or federal governmental agencies and
  3 19 private nonprofit organizations in the administration of a
  3 20 program or service receiving a grant under the initiative and
  3 21 in expenditure of grant funds awarded.
  3 22    As a condition of receiving a grant from the initiative,
  3 23 a public transit system must provide the department with
  3 24 information on any program or service for which the public
  3 25 transit system is awarded a grant. The bill requires the
  3 26 department to submit an annual report to the general assembly
  3 27 on the outcomes of the initiative, including the grant amount,
  3 28 the type of program or service receiving funds, and the number
  3 29 of individuals served for each grant awarded by the initiative.
  3 30    The bill requires the department to adopt administrative
  3 31 rules to administer the initiative, including but not limited
  3 32 to an application process and grant award criteria.
  3 33    The bill creates an employment and education rides fund in
  3 34 the state treasury under the control of the department. The
  3 35 fund shall consist of moneys appropriated to the department and
  4  1 any other moneys available to, obtained, or accepted by the
  4  2 department for placement in the fund.  The moneys in the fund
  4  3 shall be used to provide grants under the Iowa employment and
  4  4 education rides initiative established in the bill.

  3  7 transit services accessible to the general public and receiving

  3 14 operational costs directly associated with providing employment

  3 16 expenditures or construction.

  3 18 with other local, state, or federal governmental agencies and
