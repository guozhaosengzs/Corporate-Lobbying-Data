  1  9    Mental health information relating to an individual may be
  1 10 disclosed by mental health professionals to a law enforcement
  1 11 professional if and to the extent necessary to prevent a
  1 12 serious and imminent threat to the health or safety of the
  1 13 individual or others.
  1 14                           EXPLANATION
  1 15 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 16 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 17    This bill relates to the disclosure of mental health
  1 18 information to law enforcement professionals.
  1 19    Under Code chapter 228, a mental health professional, data
  1 20 collector, or employee or agent thereof, is prohibited from
  1 21 disclosing or allowing the disclosure of an individual's
  1 22 mental health information without the individual's consent or
  1 23 written authorization.  However, disclosure of such mental
  1 24 health information without the individual's consent or written
  1 25 authorization is allowed under certain circumstances, including
  1 26 for certain administrative disclosures to other mental health
  1 27 providers for administrative and professional services to
  1 28 the individual and to meet certain compulsory disclosure
  1 29 requirements pursuant to state or federal law.  In addition,
  1 30 the disclosure of certain limited mental health information is
  1 31 allowed to authorized family members without the individual's
  1 32 consent or written authorization in some circumstances.
  1 33    The bill allows for the disclosure of mental health
  1 34 information by a mental health professional relating to
  1 35 an individual without the individual's consent or written
  2  1 permission to a law enforcement professional to the extent
  2  2 necessary to prevent a serious and imminent threat to the
  2  3 health and safety of the individual or others.
  2  4    The bill defines "law enforcement professional" to mean
  2  5 a law enforcement officer as defined in Code section 80B.3
  2  6 (an officer appointed by the director of the department of
  2  7 natural resources, a member of the police force or other
  2  8 agency or department of the state, county, city, or tribal
  2  9 government regularly employed as such and who is responsible
  2 10 for the prevention and detection of crime and the enforcement
  2 11 of the criminal laws of this state and all individuals, as
  2 12 determined by the council, who by the nature of their duties
  2 13 may be required to perform the duties of a peace officer),
  2 14 county attorney as defined in Code section 331.101 (the
  2 15 county attorney, a deputy county attorney or an assistant
  2 16 county attorney designated by the county attorney), probation
  2 17 or parole officer, or jailer.  "Mental health information"
  2 18 is defined in Code section 228.1 to mean oral, written,
  2 19 or recorded information which indicates the identity of an
  2 20 individual receiving professional services and which relates to
  2 21 the diagnosis, course, or treatment of the individual's mental
  2 22 or emotional condition.
