  1 21 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 22 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 23    This bill relates to the limitations of criminal actions in
  1 24 kidnapping offenses.
  1 25    The bill provides that an information or indictment for
  1 26 kidnapping in the first, second, or third degree, committed on
  1 27 or with a person who is 18 years of age or older shall be found
  1 28 within 10 years after its commission, or if the person against
  1 29 whom the information or indictment is sought is identified
  1 30 through the use of a DNA profile for any such kidnapping
  1 31 offense committed on or with a person who is 18 years of age
  1 32 or older, the information or indictment shall be found within
  1 33 three years from the date the person is identified by the
  1 34 person's DNA profile, whichever is later. Under current law,
  1 35 an information or indictment for any such kidnapping offense
  2  1 committed on or with a person who is 18 years of age or older
  2  2 shall be found within three years after its commission.
  2  3    The bill does not change current law which provides that
  2  4 if such a kidnapping offense is committed on or with a person
  2  5 who is under 18 years of age, the information or indictment
  2  6 shall be found within 10 years after the person upon whom the
  2  7 kidnapping is committed attains 18 years of age or if the
  2  8 person against whom the information or indictment is sought is
  2  9 identified through the use of a DNA profile, an information or
  2 10 indictment shall be found within three years from the date the
  2 11 person is identified by the person's DNA profile, whichever is
  2 12 later.
  2 13    Kidnapping in the first degree is a class "A" felony
  2 14 punishable by confinement for life without possibility of
  2 15 parole for adult offenders. Kidnapping in the second degree
  2 16 is a class "B" felony (70 percent sentence) punishable by
  2 17 confinement for no more than 25 years. Kidnapping in the third
  2 18 degree is a class "C" felony punishable by confinement for no
  2 19 more than 10 years and a fine of at least $1,000 but not more
  2 20 than $10,000.
