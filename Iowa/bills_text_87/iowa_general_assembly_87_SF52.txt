  1  1    Section 1.    1  1    Section 1.    1  2 prohibited ==== penalties.
  1  3    1.  A person shall not knowingly acquire, provide, receive,
  1  4 otherwise transfer, or use a fetal body part in this state,
  1  5 regardless of whether the acquisition, provision, receipt,
  1  6 transfer, or use is for valuable consideration.
  1  7    2.  Subsection 1 shall not apply to any of the following:
  1  8    a.  Diagnostic or remedial tests, procedures, or observations
  1  9 which have the sole purpose of determining the life or health
  1 10 of the fetus in order to provide that information to the
  1 11 pregnant woman or to preserve the life or health of the fetus
  1 12 or pregnant woman.
  1 13    b.  The actions of a person taken in the furtherance of the
  1 14 final disposition of a fetal body part.
  1 15    3.  A person who violates this section is guilty of a class
  1 16 "C" felony.
  1 17    4.  For the purposes of this section:
  1 18    a.  "Abortion" means as defined in section 146.1.
  1 19    b.  "Fetal body part" means a cell, tissue, organ, or other
  1 20 part of a fetus that is terminated by an abortion.
  1 21    c.  "Final disposition" means the disposition of fetal
  1 22 body parts by burial, interment, entombment, cremation, or
  1 23 incineration.
  1 24    d.  "Valuable consideration" means any payment including but
  1 25 not limited to payment associated with the transportation,
  1 26 processing, preservation, quality control, or storage of fetal
  1 27 body parts.

  2  7    A person who violates the bill is guilty of a class "C"
  2  8 felony.  A class "C" felony is punishable by confinement for no
  2  9 more than 10 years and a fine of at least $1,000 but not more
  2 10 than $10,000.
