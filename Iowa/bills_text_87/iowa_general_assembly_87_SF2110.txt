  2 27    Under current law, an individual is disqualified from
  2 28 eligibility for unemployment benefits if the department of
  2 29 workforce development finds that the individual has been
  2 30 discharged for misconduct in connection with the individual's
  2 31 employment. "Misconduct" is defined by the department by rule.
  2 32    This bill provides that misconduct is deemed to have
  2 33 occurred in circumstances including but not limited to certain
  2 34 circumstances specified in the bill. The circumstances
  2 35 include the individual losing employment as a result of the
  3  1 individual's failure to obey a rule, order, or instruction of
  3  2 the employer; the individual losing employment as a result of
  3  3 the individual's failure to discharge the duties for which the
  3  4 individual was employed; and the individual losing employment
  3  5 as a result of the individual's disregard of the employer's
  3  6 interests or the individual's duties or obligations to the
  3  7 employer.
  3  8    The bill also prohibits the department from considering
  3  9 whether, to what extent, or in what manner an employer imposed
  3 10 disciplinary action upon an individual for any past misconduct
  3 11 by the individual when making a determination as to whether the
  3 12 individual is disqualified for benefits due to being discharged
  3 13 for misconduct.
