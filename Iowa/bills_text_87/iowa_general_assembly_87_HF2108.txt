  1  1    Section 1.  
  1  1    Section 1.    1  1    Section 1.    1  2    1.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise
  1  3 requires:
  1  4    a.  "Bait an area" means to transport feed to an area and
  1  5 place, expose, deposit, distribute, concentrate, or scatter it
  1  6 with the intent to lure, attract, or entice deer to a specific
  1  7 location.
  1  8    b.  "Baited area" means any area where feed is transported to
  1  9 and placed, exposed, deposited, distributed, concentrated, or
  1 10 scattered with the intent to lure, attract, or entice deer to a
  1 11 specific location.
  1 12    c.  "Feed" means grains, fruits, vegetables, nuts, hay,
  1 13 or any other natural or artificial products containing food
  1 14 additives or materials.  "Feed" does not include minerals unless
  1 15 those minerals contain feed or food by=products.
  1 16    d.  "Mineral site" means any area where minerals have been
  1 17 transported to and placed, including areas where minerals were
  1 18 previously transported to and leached into the soil.
  1 19    2.  A person shall not hunt, take, or attempt to take deer
  1 20 in a baited area or within two hundred yards of a baited area
  1 21 unless the feed has been gone from the baited area for at least
  1 22 ten days.
  1 23    3.  A person shall not place a tree stand, blind, or any
  1 24 other permanent hunting shelters or stands within fifty yards
  1 25 of a mineral site, and a person shall not hunt, take, or
  1 26 attempt to take deer within fifty yards of a mineral site.
  1 27    4.  Spilled, scattered, or exposed feed as the result
  1 28 of normal agricultural practices and the establishment and
  1 29 maintenance of food plots is not considered baiting.
  1 30    5.  A person shall not bait an area on public property, or
  1 31 place minerals on public property.
  1 32                           EXPLANATION
  1 33 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 34 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 35    This bill relates to the baiting of deer.
  2  1 The bill defines what a baited area is, relevant to deer,
  2  2 and prohibits hunting, taking, or attempting to take deer in
  2  3 a baited area or within 200 yards of a baited area. The bill
  2  4 also defines what a mineral site is and prohibits placing tree
  2  5 stands, blinds, or any other permanent hunting shelters or
  2  6 stands within 50 yards of a mineral site.  It also prohibits
  2  7 hunting, taking, or attempting to take deer within 50 yards
  2  8 of a mineral site. The bill prohibits all baiting on public
  2  9 property and prohibits placing of minerals on public property.
  2 10 For purposes of this bill, "feed" includes grains, fruits,
  2 11 vegetables, nuts, hay, or any other natural or artificial
  2 12 products containing food additives or materials, but does not
  2 13 include minerals unless those minerals contain feed or food
  2 14 by=products. A person who violates the provisions of the bill
  2 15 is subject to penalties pursuant to Code section 481A.135.

  1 34 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 35    This bill relates to the baiting of deer.
  2  1 The bill defines what a baited area is, relevant to deer,
  2  2 and prohibits hunting, taking, or attempting to take deer in
  2  3 a baited area or within 200 yards of a baited area. The bill
  2  4 also defines what a mineral site is and prohibits placing tree
  2  5 stands, blinds, or any other permanent hunting shelters or
  2  6 stands within 50 yards of a mineral site.  It also prohibits
  2  7 hunting, taking, or attempting to take deer within 50 yards
  2  8 of a mineral site. The bill prohibits all baiting on public
  2  9 property and prohibits placing of minerals on public property.
  2 10 For purposes of this bill, "feed" includes grains, fruits,
  2 11 vegetables, nuts, hay, or any other natural or artificial
  2 12 products containing food additives or materials, but does not
  2 13 include minerals unless those minerals contain feed or food
  2 14 by=products. A person who violates the provisions of the bill
  2 15 is subject to penalties pursuant to Code section 481A.135.
       LSB 5555YH (3) 87
       js/rn
  1 12    c.  "Feed" means grains, fruits, vegetables, nuts, hay,
  1 13 or any other natural or artificial products containing food
  1 14 additives or materials.  "Feed" does not include minerals unless
  1 15 those minerals contain feed or food by=products.
  1 16    d.  "Mineral site" means any area where minerals have been
  1 17 transported to and placed, including areas where minerals were
  1 18 previously transported to and leached into the soil.
  1 19    2.  A person shall not hunt, take, or attempt to take deer
  1 20 in a baited area or within two hundred yards of a baited area
  1 21 unless the feed has been gone from the baited area for at least
  1 22 ten days.
  1 23    3.  A person shall not place a tree stand, blind, or any
  1 24 other permanent hunting shelters or stands within fifty yards
  1 25 of a mineral site, and a person shall not hunt, take, or
  1 26 attempt to take deer within fifty yards of a mineral site.
  1 27    4.  Spilled, scattered, or exposed feed as the result
  1 28 of normal agricultural practices and the establishment and
  1 29 maintenance of food plots is not considered baiting.
  1 30    5.  A person shall not bait an area on public property, or
  1 31 place minerals on public property.
