  2  6 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  2  7 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  2  8    This bill provides that, in addition to payment by the
  2  9 state of fees and expenses of a county medical examiner who
  2 10 performs an autopsy or conducts an investigation of a person
  2 11 who died after being brought into the state for emergency
  2 12 medical treatment by or at the direction of an out=of=state
  2 13 law enforcement officer or public authority, the state is
  2 14 also required to pay such fees and expenses applicable to any
  2 15 other decedent who is an out=of=state resident and dies after
  2 16 entering the state.
  2 17    The bill provides that a claim for payment by the state shall
  2 18 be filed with the state appeal board and if the claim meets the
  2 19 criterion specified the claim shall be authorized by the board
  2 20 and paid out of moneys of the general fund of the state not
  2 21 otherwise appropriated. A claim for payment shall not affect
  2 22 or delay payment of the state medical examiner or of the county
  2 23 medical examiner or other personnel of the office of the county
  2 24 medical examiner.
  2 25    For the purposes of the bill, "county medical examiner"
  2 26 includes the state medical examiner, deputy state medical
  2 27 examiner, associate state medical examiner, or deputy county
  2 28 medical examiner when acting as the county medical examiner
  2 29 or a county medical examiner investigator. "Out=of=state
  2 30 resident" means an individual who is not a resident of the
  2 31 state of Iowa and includes an individual who is not a citizen
  2 32 of the United States nor lawfully admitted into the United
  2 33 States for permanent residence by the United States immigration
  2 34 and naturalization service.
  2 35    The bill directs the state medical examiner, subject to the
  3  1 approval of the director of public health, to adopt rules to
  3  2 implement the bill.
