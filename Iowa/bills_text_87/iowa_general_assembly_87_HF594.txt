  2 14 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  2 15 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  2 16    BILL'S PROVISIONS.  This bill provides that a research
  2 17 facility must establish a program to transfer dogs and
  2 18 cats which are no longer used for testing, evaluation, or
  2 19 observation to an animal shelter or animal rescue organization.
  2 20 The bill defines an animal rescue organization as a nonprofit
  2 21 entity that holds animals on a short=term basis and is not
  2 22 engaged in the destruction of animals. As part of the program,
  2 23 the research facility may transfer the animal to a member of
  2 24 the public for adoption.
  2 25    Finally, the bill provides that a research facility
  2 26 providing for the transfer of a dog or cat under its program is
  2 27 not liable for civil damages caused by the animal's condition
  2 28 or behavior unless such transfer constitutes gross negligence.
  2 29    BACKGROUND.  A research facility is a public or private
  2 30 school, college, or scientific institution concerned with the
  2 31 investigation of, or instruction concerning the structure
  2 32 or function of living organisms (Code section 162.2). The
  2 33 facility is regulated by the department of agriculture and land
  2 34 stewardship (DALS) as a commercial establishment and operates
  2 35 pursuant to a certificate of registration obtained from the
  3  1 department (Code section 162.4A).
  3  2    APPLICABLE CIVIL PENALTIES.  DALS is required to establish,
  3  3 impose, and assess civil penalties for violations of Code
  3  4 chapter 162 (Code section 162.12A).  Generally, the civil
  3  5 penalty cannot exceed $500 and each day that a violation
  3  6 continues constitutes a separate offense.
