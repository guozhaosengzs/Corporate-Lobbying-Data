  1 12 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 13 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 14    This bill provides that a person commits a class "C" felony
  1 15 when the person unintentionally causes the death of another by
  1 16 exceeding a speed limit established or posted pursuant to Code
  1 17 section 321.236, subsection 13, paragraph "a" (powers of local
  1 18 authorities to regulate speed in rural residence districts),
  1 19 Code section 321.285 (general speed restrictions), Code
  1 20 section 321.288, subsection 2, paragraph "f" (reduced speed
  1 21 in road work zones), or Code section 321.289 (speed signs),
  1 22 321.290 (special restrictions), 321.293 (local authorities may
  1 23 alter limits), or 321.295 (limitation on bridge or elevated
  1 24 structures), by 25 miles per hour or more, if the death of the
  1 25 other person directly or indirectly results from the violation.
  1 26 A class "C" felony is punishable by imprisonment for no more
  1 27 than 10 years and a fine of at least $1,000 but not more than
  1 28 $10,000. The bill exempts members of a public safety agency
  1 29 performing official duties from the provision.
  1 30    By operation of law, a person who commits a violation
  1 31 under the bill is subject to driver's license suspension for
  1 32 vehicular homicide (Code section 321.210D) and is disqualified
  1 33 from operating a commercial motor vehicle for one year (Code
  1 34 section 321.208). In addition, the person is prohibited from
  1 35 being admitted to bail during the appeal process (Code section
  2  1 811.1). If the person fails to remain at the scene of the
  2  2 accident in violation of Code section 321.261, the person
  2  3 must also be denied parole or work release until the person
  2  4 has served at least seven=tenths of the maximum term of the
  2  5 person's sentence (Code section 902.12).
