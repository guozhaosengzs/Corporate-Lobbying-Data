  1 21 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 22 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 23    Current law allows a city to propose the establishment of
  1 24 a utility board, subject to the approval of the voters of
  1 25 the city, with either three or five members. If a proposal
  1 26 receives a favorable majority vote, the mayor shall appoint the
  1 27 board members as provided in the proposal. This bill provides
  1 28 that notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary,
  1 29 a city council may by ordinance or resolution increase the
  1 30 number of members on a utility board from three members to five
  1 31 members. Upon the adoption of such ordinance or resolution,
  1 32 vacant positions shall be filled pursuant to Code section
