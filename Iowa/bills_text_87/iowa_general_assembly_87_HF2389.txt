  1  3    1.  A person commits the offense of electronic handling
  1  4 device tampering if all of the following apply:
  1  5    a.  The person knowingly removes, disables, or destroys
  1  6 an electric device designed and used to maintain custody or
  1  7 control of the dog or modify the dog's behavior.
  1  8    b.  The electronic device is attached to or worn by the
  1  9 dog or attached to an item worn by the dog, including but not
  1 10 limited to a collar, harness, or vest.
  1 11    2.  a.  For the first conviction, the person is guilty of a
  1 12 simple misdemeanor.
  1 13    b.  For a second or subsequent conviction, the person is
  1 14 guilty of a serious misdemeanor.
  1 15    3.  This section shall not apply to an act taken by any of
  1 16 the following:
  1 17    a.  The owner of the dog, an agent of the owner, or a person
  1 18 authorized to take action by the owner.
  1 19    b.  A peace officer as defined in section 801.4.
  1 20    c.  A veterinarian licensed as provided in chapter 169.
  1 21                           EXPLANATION
  1 22 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 23 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 24    This bill prohibits a person from knowingly tampering with
  1 25 an electronic handling device that is designed and used to
  1 26 maintain custody or control of the dog or modify the dog's
  1 27 behavior.  The tampering must be an act that removes, disables,
  1 28 or destroys the device while attached to the dog or attached
  1 29 to an item worn by the dog.  The offense does not apply to
  1 30 the dog's owner or to an agent of or a person authorized to
  1 31 take action by the owner, a peace officer, or a licensed
  1 32 veterinarian.
  1 33    For the first conviction, a person is guilty of a simple
  1 34 misdemeanor and for a second or subsequent conviction, a person
  1 35 is guilty of a serious misdemeanor.
  2  1 A simple misdemeanor is punishable by confinement for no
  2  2 more than 30 days or a fine of at least $65 but not more than
  2  3 $625 or by both. A serious misdemeanor is punishable by
  2  4 confinement for no more than one year and a fine of at least
  2  5 $315 but not more than $1,875.
