 A BILL FOR

  1 An Act relating to supplemental reimbursement for publicly
  2    owned or operated ground emergency medical transportation
  3    providers through the Medicaid program, and including
  4    effective date provisions.
  5 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF IOWA:
    TLSB 5688YH (6) 87
    pf/rh

PAG LIN



  1  1    Section 1.  DEPARTMENT OF HUMAN SERVICES ==== SUBMISSION OF
  1  2 MEDICAID STATE PLAN AMENDMENT ==== SUPPLEMENTAL REIMBURSEMENT FOR
  1  3 ELIGIBLE GROUND EMERGENCY MEDICAL TRANSPORTATION SERVICES.
  1  4    1.  By July 1, 2018, the department of human services
  1  5 shall submit a Medicaid state plan amendment to the
  1  6 centers for Medicare and Medicaid services of the United
  1  7 States department of health and human services to request
  1  8 authorization to establish and administer a methodology to
  1  9 provide supplemental reimbursement to eligible ground emergency
  1 10 medical transportation providers that provide ground emergency
  1 11 medical transportation services to Medicaid beneficiaries.
  1 12 Supplemental reimbursements shall be available only for the
  1 13 uncompensated care costs incurred by eligible ground emergency
  1 14 medical transportation providers for providing ground emergency
  1 15 medical transportation  services to Medicaid beneficiaries.
  1 16 The department shall implement applicability of the
  1 17 supplemental reimbursement to eligible ground emergency medical
  1 18 transportation providers upon receipt of federal approval.
  1 19    2.  For the purposes of this section:
  1 20    a.  "Advanced life support" means special services designed
  1 21 to provide definitive prehospital emergency medical care,
  1 22 including but not limited to cardiopulmonary resuscitation,
  1 23 cardiac monitoring, cardiac defibrillation, advanced airway
  1 24 management, intravenous therapy, administration of drugs and
  1 25 other medicinal preparations, and other specified techniques
  1 26 and procedures.
  1 27    b.  "Basic life support" means emergency first aid and
  1 28 cardiopulmonary resuscitation procedures to maintain life
  1 29 without invasive techniques.
  1 30    c.  "Eligible ground emergency medical transportation
  1 31 provider" means a provider who is eligible to receive
  1 32 supplemental payments, provides ground emergency medical
  1 33 transportation services to Medicaid beneficiaries continuously
  1 34 during the claiming period, is enrolled as a Medicaid provider
  1 35 during the period being claimed, and meets one of the following
  2  1 conditions:
  2  2    (1)  Is owned or operated by an eligible governmental entity,
  2  3 including but not limited to the state, a city, county, city
  2  4 and county, or fire district.
  2  5    (2)  Contracts with an eligible governmental entity,
  2  6 including but not limited to the state, a city, county, city
  2  7 and county, or fire district.
  2  8    d.  "Ground emergency medical transportation services" means
  2  9 both the act of transporting an individual from any point of
  2 10 origin to the nearest medical facility capable of meeting the
  2 11 emergency medical needs of the individual, as well as the
  2 12 advanced life support, limited=advanced life support, and basic
  2 13 life support services provided to an individual by ground
  2 14 emergency medical transportation providers before or during the
  2 15 act of transportation.
  2 16    e.  "Limited advanced life support" means special services
  2 17 to provide prehospital emergency medical care limited to
  2 18 techniques and procedures that exceed basic life support but
  2 19 are less than advanced life support services.
  2 20    Sec. 2.  REVISION OF STATE PLAN AMENDMENT REQUEST.  If
  2 21 the centers for Medicare and Medicaid services of the United
  2 22 States department of health and human services determines that
  2 23 the  state plan amendment authorized and submitted pursuant
  2 24 to section 1 of this Act shall not apply to eligible ground
  2 25 emergency medical transportation providers who contract with
  2 26 an eligible governmental entity as specified in section 1,
  2 27 subsection 2, paragraph "c", subparagraph (2), of this Act, the
  2 28 department shall revise the state plan amendment to exclude
  2 29 such providers and such revised state plan amendment shall
  2 30 be implemented in lieu of the initial state plan amendment
  2 31 submitted upon receipt of federal approval.
  2 32    Sec. 3.  EFFECTIVE UPON ENACTMENT.  This Act, being deemed of
  2 33 immediate importance, takes effect upon enactment.
  2 34                           EXPLANATION
  2 35 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  3  1 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  3  2    This bill directs the department of human services (DHS),
  3  3 by  July 1, 2018, to submit a state plan amendment to the
  3  4 centers for Medicare and Medicaid services of the United States
  3  5 department of health and human services (CMS) to request
  3  6 authorization to establish and administer a methodology to
  3  7 provide supplemental reimbursement to eligible ground emergency
  3  8 medical transportation providers. Supplemental reimbursements
  3  9 provided by the program shall be available only for the
  3 10 uncompensated care costs incurred by eligible ground emergency
  3 11 medical transportation providers. The bill provides, however,
  3 12 that if CMS determines that the state plan amendment authorized
  3 13 and submitted under the bill shall not include, as eligible
  3 14 ground emergency medical transportation providers, those who
  3 15 contract with an eligible governmental entity as specified
  3 16 in the bill, DHS shall revise the state plan amendment to
  3 17 exclude such providers and such revised state plan amendment
  3 18 shall be implemented in lieu of the initial state plan
  3 19 amendment submitted upon receipt of federal approval. Upon
  3 20 approval of the state plan amendment, DHS shall apply the
  3 21 supplemental reimbursement to eligible ground emergency medical
  3 22 transportation providers.
  3 23    The bill takes effect upon enactment.
       LSB 5688YH (6) 87
       pf/rh
