 A BILL FOR

  1 An Act relating to required asset, income, and identity
  2    verification for the Medicaid program, and including
  3    effective date provisions.
  4 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF IOWA:
    TLSB 2251YH (2) 87
    pf/nh

PAG LIN



  1  1    Section 1.  MEDICAID PROGRAM == ASSET, INCOME, AND IDENTITY
  1  2 VERIFICATION.  The department of human services shall issue a
  1  3 request for proposals to contract with a third=party vendor
  1  4 to establish an electronic asset, income, and identity
  1  5 eligibility verification system for the purposes of determining
  1  6 or redetermining the eligibility of an individual who is
  1  7 an applicant for or recipient of medical assistance under
  1  8 the Medicaid state plan on the basis of being aged, blind,
  1  9 or disabled in accordance with 42 U.S.C. {1396w. The
  1 10 third=party vendor selected shall be able to demonstrate in
  1 11 writing its current relationships or contracts with financial
  1 12 institutions in the state and nationally. Participation by
  1 13 financial institutions in providing account balances for
  1 14 asset verification shall remain voluntary. The department of
  1 15 human services shall submit a progress report, by September
  1 16 1, 2017,  to the chairpersons and ranking members of the joint
  1 17 appropriations subcommittee on health and human services, the
  1 18 legislative services agency, and the legislative caucus staffs.
  1 19    Sec. 2.  EFFECTIVE UPON ENACTMENT.  This Act, being deemed of
  1 20 immediate importance, takes effect upon enactment.
  1 21                           EXPLANATION
  1 22 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 23 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 24    This bill requires the department of human services (DHS) to
  1 25 issue a request for proposals to contract with a third=party
  1 26 vendor to establish an electronic asset, income, and identity
  1 27 eligibility verification system to determine or redetermine
  1 28 the eligibility of an individual who is an applicant for or
  1 29 recipient of Medicaid on the basis of being aged, blind, or
  1 30 disabled. The third=party vendor selected shall be able to
  1 31 demonstrate in writing its current relationships or contracts
  1 32 with financial institutions in the state and nationally.
  1 33 Participation by financial institutions in providing account
  1 34 balances for asset verification shall remain voluntary. DHS
  1 35 is required to submit a progress report, by September 1,
  2  1 2017, to the chairpersons and ranking members of the joint
  2  2 appropriations subcommittee on health and human services, the
  2  3 legislative services agency, and the legislative caucus staffs.
  2  4    The bill takes effect upon enactment.
       LSB 2251YH (2) 87
