  1  1    Section 1.    1  1    Section 1.    1  2 candidates.
  1  3    1.  For the purposes of this section, "election campaign
  1  4 cycle" means the time period between elections for a particular
  1  5 office. Each primary, general, and special election is a
  1  6 separate election campaign cycle. Separate contribution limits
  1  7 for each type of election for a particular office, including
  1  8 primary, general, and special elections, apply in an election
  1  9 campaign cycle.
  1 10    2.  A person shall not make and a candidate, candidate's
  1 11 committee, or anyone acting on behalf of any candidate or
  1 12 candidate's committee shall not accept any contribution which
  1 13 will cause the total amount of the person's contributions to
  1 14 a candidate or candidate's committee to exceed the following
  1 15 amounts for an election campaign cycle:
  1 16    a.  From a candidate's committee, two thousand dollars.
  1 17    b.  From a political committee that contributes to five or
  1 18 more candidates, five thousand dollars.
  1 19    c.  From a political committee that contributes to fewer than
  1 20 five candidates, two thousand seven hundred dollars.
  1 21    d.  From a national political party, state statutory
  1 22 political committee, or county statutory political committee,
  1 23 five thousand dollars.
  1 24    e.  From any other person, two thousand seven hundred
  1 25 dollars.
  1 26    3.  This section applies to candidates for state senator,
  1 27 state representative, and the office of a statewide elected
  1 28 official as defined in section 68B.2.
  1 29                           EXPLANATION
  1 30 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 31 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 32    This bill imposes campaign contribution limits on candidates
  1 33 for state senator or representative and candidates for
  1 34 statewide elected office. In an election campaign cycle, a
  1 35 candidate's committee shall not contribute to a candidate more
  2  1 than $2,000; a political committee that contributes to five
  2  2 or more candidates shall not contribute more than $5,000 to
  2  3 one candidate; a political committee that contributes to fewer
  2  4 than five candidates shall not contribute more than $2,700 to
  2  5 one candidate; a national political party, state statutory
  2  6 political committee, or county statutory political committee
  2  7 shall not contribute more than $5,000; and no other person
  2  8 shall contribute more than $2,700.
  2  9    A person who violates the bill is subject to penalties
  2 10 pursuant to Code section 68B.32D.  A person who willfully
  2 11 violates this bill is guilty of a serious misdemeanor. A
  2 12 serious misdemeanor is punishable by confinement for no more
  2 13 than one year and a fine of at least $315 but not more than
  2 14 $1,875.
  2 15    A variety of civil remedies are also available in Code
  2 16 section 68B.32D for a violation of Code chapter 68A or rules
  2 17 of the ethics and campaign disclosure board, ranging from a
  2 18 reprimand to a civil penalty of not more than $2,000.
