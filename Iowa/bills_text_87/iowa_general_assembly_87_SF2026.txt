  1  3    1.  A health care provider shall disclose and make available
  1  4 to the public in a single document, either electronically or
  1  5 by posting conspicuously on the provider's internet site,
  1  6 if one exists, the prices charged, prior to the negotiation
  1  7 of any discounts and assuming no medical complications, for
  1  8 the twenty=five most common  health services the health care
  1  9 provider renders.
  1 10    a.  The health care provider shall identify the health
  1 11 service for which prices charged are provided in both a common
  1 12 procedural terminology code or other coding system commonly
  1 13 used by the health care provider and accepted as a national
  1 14 standard for billing, and in a plain language description.
  1 15    b.  The health care provider shall update the price
  1 16 information as frequently as the health care provider deems
  1 17 appropriate, but at least annually.
  1 18    2.  A hospital shall disclose and make available to the
  1 19 public in a single document, either electronically or by
  1 20 posting conspicuously on the hospital's internet site, if
  1 21 one exists, the prices charged, prior to the negotiation of
  1 22 any discounts and assuming no medical complications, for
  1 23 the  seventy=five most common inpatient health services and
  1 24 the seventy=five most common outpatient health services, as
  1 25 grouped by a Medicare diagnosis=related group, rendered by
  1 26 the hospital.  The price information shall be updated on a
  1 27 quarterly basis.
  1 28    3.  Any price information disclosed under this section shall
  1 29 include a disclaimer that the price information provided is
  1 30 an estimate, does not constitute a legally binding charge for
  1 31 a health service provided to a specific consumer, and that
  1 32 the actual charge for a health  service is dependent on the
  1 33 circumstances at the time the health service is rendered.
  1 34                           EXPLANATION
  1 35 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  2  1 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  2  2    This bill relates to the disclosure of prices charged by
  2  3 health care providers and hospitals for common health services.
  2  4    The bill requires a health care provider to disclose and
  2  5 make available to the public in a single document, either
  2  6 electronically or by posting conspicuously on the provider's
  2  7 internet site, the prices charged, prior to the negotiation of
  2  8 any discounts and assuming no medical complications, for the 25
  2  9 most common health services the health care provider renders.
  2 10 The health services are to be identified by a common nationally
  2 11 recognized coding system and by a plain language description,
  2 12 and the health care provider is required to update the price
  2 13 information at least annually.
  2 14    The bill requires hospitals to disclose and make available
  2 15 to the public in a single document, either electronically or
  2 16 by posting conspicuously on the hospital's internet site, the
  2 17 prices charged, prior to the negotiation of any discounts and
  2 18 assuming no medical complications, for the 75 most common
  2 19 inpatient and the 75 most common outpatient health services, as
  2 20 grouped by a Medicare diagnosis=related group, rendered by the
  2 21 hospital.  The price information is required to be updated on
  2 22 a quarterly basis.
  2 23    The bill provides that the price information disclosed shall
  2 24 include a disclaimer that the price information provided is
  2 25 an estimate, does not constitute a legally binding charge for
  2 26 a health service provided to a specific consumer, and that
  2 27 the actual charge for a health service is dependent on the
  2 28 circumstances at the time the service is rendered.
