  2  2                           EXPLANATION
  2  3 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  2  4 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  2  5    This bill relates to permissible interest rates and charges
  2  6 for certain loans.
  2  7    Current law allows the superintendent of banking to
  2  8 establish the maximum rate of interest or charges for regulated
  2  9 loans subject to Code chapter 536 with unpaid principal
  2 10 balances of $10,000 or less. The bill increases this amount to
  2 11 $30,000. For loans with unpaid principal balances in excess
  2 12 of $30,000 (formerly $10,000), the maximum interest rate or
  2 13 charges a lender may charge remains the greater of the rate
  2 14 permitted in Code chapter 535 or the rate authorized for
  2 15 supervised financial organizations in Code chapter 537.
  2 16    The bill authorizes a creditor to contract for and receive,
  2 17 for an interest=bearing consumer credit transaction, a service
  2 18 charge in an amount not to exceed the lesser of 10 percent
  2 19 of the amount financed or $30. The bill provides that if a
  2 20 creditor has received such a service charge, the creditor
  2 21 shall not collect or retain a minimum charge upon prepayment
  2 22 as authorized under Code section 537.2510. The bill specifies
  2 23 that Code section 537.2510, relating to rebate upon prepayment,
  2 24 does not apply to service charges collected pursuant to the
  2 25 bill.
