  1  1    Section 1.    1  1    Section 1.    1  2 recovery monitoring system.
  1  3    1.  The division in cooperation with the center shall
  1  4 establish a statewide soil resource health and recovery
  1  5 monitoring system that receives data regarding the continued
  1  6 capacity of this state's soils to permanently sustain plant and
  1  7 animal life.  As part of the program, the division shall adopt
  1  8 long=term objectives and short=term interim goals or benchmarks
  1  9 on a statewide and regional basis.
  1 10    2.  The system shall provide for methods to measure, analyze,
  1 11 and track all of the following:
  1 12    a.  A soil's nutrient retention capacity and a soil's
  1 13 fertility.
  1 14    b.  The structure, stability, and compaction of a soil.
  1 15    c.  Soil depth and degrees of soil erosion.
  1 16    d.  The capacity of a soil to retain precipitation and its
  1 17 capacity to filter the transport of nutrients.
  1 18    e.  A soil's habitat for earthworms and soil microbes.
  1 19    f.  The value of a soil to produce crops, and levels of
  1 20 nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium, magnesium, sulfur, and calcium
  1 21 present in a soil.
  1 22    3.  The division in cooperation with the center shall do all
  1 23 of the following:
  1 24    a.  Evaluate its progress in administering the program to
  1 25 ensure its long=term viability and improvement.
  1 26    b.  Collaborate with participating landowners.  The division
  1 27 may enter into maintenance agreements or easements with
  1 28 participating landowners.
  1 29    4.  a.  Beginning December 14, 2018, and each two years
  1 30 after that date, the division in cooperation with the center
  1 31 shall submit a soil resource health and recovery report to the
  1 32 governor and general assembly.
  1 33    b.  The report shall provide a summary of the division's
  1 34 activities, findings, and recommendations to sustain and
  1 35 improve this state's soil resources, including methods to
  2  1 assist in the recovery of soil health.  The recommendation
  2  2 shall include proposed legislation or administrative rules.
  2  3                           EXPLANATION
  2  4 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  2  5 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  2  6    This bill requires the division of soil conservation
  2  7 and water quality of the department of agriculture and land
  2  8 stewardship, in cooperation with the Iowa nutrient research
  2  9 center associated with Iowa state university of science and
  2 10 technology, to establish a statewide soil resources health and
  2 11 recovery monitoring system in order to measure, analyze, and
  2 12 track soil health regarding the continued capacity of this
  2 13 state's soils to permanently sustain plant and animal life.
  2 14 The division and center must collaborate with participating
  2 15 landowners.  The division and center must submit a report to
  2 16 the governor and general assembly each two years, beginning
  2 17 December 14, 2018, that summarizes the division's activities,
  2 18 findings, and recommendations.
