  1  4 requires:
  1  5    a.  "Graduates available for employment" means the number
  1  6 of graduates minus the number of graduates unavailable for
  1  7 employment.
  1  8    b.  "Graduates employed in the field" means graduates who
  1  9 are gainfully employed in a single position for which the
  1 10 institution represents the program prepares its graduates,
  1 11 beginning within six months after a student completes the
  1 12 applicable degree or certificate program. For occupations for
  1 13 which the state requires passing an examination, the period of
  1 14 employment shall begin within six months of the announcement
  1 15 of the examination results for the first examination available
  1 16 after a student completes an applicable educational program.
  1 17    c.  "Graduates unavailable for employment" means graduates
  1 18 who, after graduation, die, become incarcerated, are called to
  1 19 active military duty, are international students who leave the
  1 20 United States or do not have a visa allowing employment in the
  1 21 United States, or are enrolled at an accredited postsecondary
  1 22 institution.
  1 23    2.  Each community college shall annually compile and make
  1 24 electronically accessible to each enrolled student a graduate
  1 25 outcomes report that includes data on the employment of and
  1 26 salaries earned by graduates of each degree or certificate
  1 27 program the community college offered during the prior school
  1 28 year.
  1 29    3.  The graduate outcomes report shall include, at a minimum,
  1 30 the following:
  1 31    a.  The job placement rate, calculated by dividing the
  1 32 number of graduates employed in the field by the number of
  1 33 graduates available for employment for each program that is
  1 34 either designed or advertised to lead to a particular career or
  1 35 which is advertised or promoted with any claim regarding job
  2  1 placement.
  2  2    b.  Salary and wage information, consisting of the total
  2  3 number of graduates employed in the field and the annual wages
  2  4 or salaries of those graduates stated in increments of five
  2  5 thousand dollars.
  2  6    4.  Each community college shall maintain each year's
  2  7 graduate outcomes report and make it electronically accessible
  2  8 to the public for not less than five years from the date of the
  2  9 publication of the rates and information.

  3 10 private institution, and regents university to annually
  3 11 compile and make electronically accessible to each enrolled
  3 12 student a graduate outcomes report that includes data on the
  3 13 employment of and salaries earned by graduates of each degree
  3 14 or certificate program the public or private postsecondary
  3 15 educational institution offered during the prior school year.
  3 16    The graduate outcomes report must include, at a minimum, the
  3 17 job placement rate and salary and wage information consisting
  3 18 of the total number of graduates employed in the field and
  3 19 the annual wages or salaries of those graduates stated in
  3 20 increments of $5,000.
  3 21    Under the bill, "graduates available for employment" is
  3 22 the number of graduates minus the number of graduates who are
  3 23 unavailable for employment due to death, incarceration, active
  3 24 military duty, departure from the United States, or enrollment
  3 25 at an accredited postsecondary institution.  "Graduates
  3 26 employed in the field" are graduates who are gainfully employed
  3 27 in a single position for which the institution represents the
  3 28 program prepares its graduates, beginning within six months
  3 29 after a student completes the applicable degree or certificate
  3 30 program. For occupations for which the state requires passing
  3 31 an examination, the period of employment shall begin within
  3 32 six months of the announcement of the examination results for
  3 33 the first examination available after a student completes an
  3 34 applicable educational program.
  3 35    Each report must be retained by the institution and
  4  1 electronically accessible to the public for five years from the
  4  2 date of the publication of the rates and information.

  4  4 accessibility by students to graduate outcomes reports is
