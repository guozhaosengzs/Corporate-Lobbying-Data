  1  5 vehicle shall approach the vehicle with due caution and shall
  1  6 proceed in one of the following manners, absent any other
  1  7 direction by a peace officer:
  1  8    a.  Make a lane change into a lane not adjacent to the
  1  9 garbage or recycling collection vehicle if possible in the
  1 10 existing safety and traffic conditions.
  1 11    b.  If a lane change under paragraph "a" would be impossible,
  1 12 prohibited by law, or unsafe, reduce the speed of the motor
  1 13 vehicle to a reasonable and proper speed for the existing road
  1 14 and traffic conditions, which speed shall be less than the
  1 15 posted speed limit, and be prepared to stop.
  1 16                           EXPLANATION
  1 17 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 18 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 19    This bill requires the operator of a motor vehicle
  1 20 approaching a stationary garbage or recycling collection
  1 21 vehicle to approach the vehicle with due caution and to make
  1 22 a lane change into a lane not adjacent to the garbage or
  1 23 recycling collection vehicle if possible in the existing
  1 24 safety and traffic conditions, or if a lane change would be
  1 25 impossible, prohibited by law, or unsafe, reduce the speed
  1 26 of the vehicle to a reasonable and proper speed for the
  1 27 existing road and traffic conditions, which speed must be
  1 28 less than the posted speed limit, and be prepared to stop.
  1 29 These requirements exist under current law for motor vehicles
  1 30 approaching stationary authorized emergency vehicles, towing
  1 31 or recovery vehicles, utility maintenance vehicles, municipal
  1 32 maintenance vehicles, and highway maintenance vehicles.
  1 33    A violation of the bill is punishable by a scheduled fine of
  1 34 $100. In addition, a person convicted of a violation resulting
  1 35 in an accident causing property damage may be subject to the
  2  1 suspension of the person's driver's license for 90 days. A
  2  2 person convicted of a violation resulting in an accident
  2  3 causing bodily injury to another person may be subject to a
  2  4 fine of $500 and suspension of the person's driver's license
  2  5 for 180 days. A person convicted of a violation resulting in
  2  6 an accident causing the death of another person may be subject
  2  7 to a fine of $1,000 and suspension of the person's driver's
  2  8 license for one year.
