  1 16 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 17 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 18    Under current law, Code section 321J.2 (operating a motor
  1 19 vehicle while under the influence of alcohol or a drug or while
  1 20 having an alcohol concentration of .08 or more) provides that
  1 21 in determining whether an operating=while=intoxicated violation
  1 22 charged is a second or subsequent offense, any conviction
  1 23 or revocation deleted from motor vehicle operating records
  1 24 pursuant to Code section 321.12 is prohibited from being
  1 25 considered as a previous offense. Code section 321.12 requires
  1 26 the department of transportation to destroy motor vehicle
  1 27 operating records pertaining to arrests, convictions, and
  1 28 driver's license revocations for operating=while=intoxicated
  1 29 offenses 12 years after the date of conviction or the effective
  1 30 date of revocation unless the operating record is subject to
  1 31 federal commercial motor vehicle regulations.
  1 32    This bill amends Code section 321.12 to increase the record
  1 33 retention period from 12 years to 25 years.
