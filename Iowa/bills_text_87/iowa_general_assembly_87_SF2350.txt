  5 32 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  5 33 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  5 34    This bill combines and repeals certain educational
  5 35 assistance programs administered by the college student aid
  6  1 commission for the benefit of registered nurses, physician
  6  2 assistants, advanced registered nurse practitioners, doctors of
  6  3 nursing practice, and nurse educators.
  6  4    RURAL IOWA ADVANCED REGISTERED NURSE PRACTITIONER AND
  6  5 PHYSICIAN ASSISTANT LOAN REPAYMENT PROGRAM ==== FUNDS.  The bill
  6  6 modifies the rural Iowa advanced registered nurse practitioner
  6  7 and physician assistant loan repayment program by prohibiting
  6  8 the commission from entering into any new program agreements on
  6  9 or after July 1, 2018; striking a provision to allow a person
  6 10 who entered into a program agreement to practice part=time;
  6 11 repealing the program July 1, 2023; and providing for the
  6 12 transfer of any balance in the rural Iowa advanced registered
  6 13 nurse practitioner and physician assistant trust fund on June
  6 14 30, 2023, to the health care loan repayment fund established
  6 15 for purposes of the health care loan repayment program.
  6 16    COMBINED LOAN PROGRAM.  The bill expands the current
  6 17 registered nurse and nurse educator program by adding advanced
  6 18 registered nurse practitioners, doctors of nursing practice,
  6 19 and physician assistants to the program.  The bill changes the
  6 20 name of the program, and of the program fund, to the health
  6 21 care loan repayment program and the health care loan repayment
  6 22 fund.
  6 23    To qualify for the program, applicants who are registered
  6 24 nurses, advanced registered nurse practitioners, doctors of
  6 25 nursing practice, or physician assistants must complete and
  6 26 return an affidavit of practice verifying that the applicant is
  6 27 or will be practicing full=time in a service commitment area
  6 28 in this state or is a nurse educator teaching full=time in
  6 29 this state.  The affidavit must specify the service commitment
  6 30 area in which the applicant will practice as a condition of
  6 31 receiving loan repayment.  Nurse educators are not required
  6 32 to teach in service commitment areas.  An applicant who is a
  6 33 member of the Iowa national guard is exempt from the service
  6 34 commitment area requirement, but must submit an affidavit
  6 35 verifying the applicant is practicing in this state.  The bill
  7  1 defines a service commitment area as a city in Iowa with a
  7  2 population of less than 26,000 that is located more than 50
  7  3 miles from a city with a population of 50,000 or more.
  7  4    Currently, the annual amount of loan repayment for a
  7  5 registered nurse or nurse educator shall not exceed the
  7  6 resident tuition rate established for regents universities or
  7  7 20 percent of the registered nurse's total federally determined
  7  8 loan amount, including principal and interest, whichever
  7  9 amount is less.  The bill limits the annual amount of loan
  7 10 repayment to a program applicant to $6,000 or 20 percent of
  7 11 the recipient's total qualified student loan, whichever amount
  7 12 is less. The bill defines qualified student loan as a loan
  7 13 that was made, insured, or guaranteed under Tit. IV of the
  7 14 federal Higher Education Act of 1965, as amended, or under
  7 15 Tit. VII or VIII of the federal Public Health Service Act, as
  7 16 amended, directly to the borrower for attendance at an approved
  7 17 postsecondary educational institution.
  7 18    The commission is required to establish by rule the criteria
  7 19 to be used in evaluating applications, and to give highest
  7 20 priority to applicants who are residents of Iowa, then, if
  7 21 requested by the adjutant general, to applicants who are
  7 22 members of the Iowa national guard.
