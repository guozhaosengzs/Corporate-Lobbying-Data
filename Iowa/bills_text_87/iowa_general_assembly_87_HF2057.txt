  4 35 may sell milk or a milk product that is unpasteurized or
  5  1 ungraded, if it is produced at that dairy farm and sold to an
  5  2 individual as commercial feed for animals.  The person would
  5  3 not be required to obtain a permit or comply with inspection
  5  4 requirements as otherwise provided in the "Iowa Grade 'A' Milk
  5  5 Inspection Law" (Code chapter 192).  The person also would
  5  6 not be required to obtain a license or comply with inspection
  5  7 requirements under the "Iowa Commercial Feed Law" (Code chapter
  5  8 198).  The bill requires that a container holding unpasteurized
  5  9 or ungraded milk or a milk product be conspicuously labeled
  5 10 (Code section 191.2).  A person who purchases milk or a milk
  5 11 product for commercial feed for resale is guilty of a simple
  5 12 misdemeanor (new Code section 192.113A).  A simple misdemeanor
  5 13 is punishable by confinement for no more than 30 days or a fine
  5 14 of at least $65 but not more than $625 or by both.
