  1 14    This bill relates to an ex parte court order authorizing
  1 15 the interception of wire, oral, or electronic communications
  1 16 by special state agents.
  1 17    The bill specifies that upon application, the court may
  1 18 enter an ex parte order, authorizing the interception of wire,
  1 19 oral, or electronic communications if the court finds there is
  1 20 probable cause for belief that an individual is committing, has
  1 21 committed, or is about to commit a felony offense involving
  1 22 any of the following: a forcible felony, a felony offense
  1 23 involving ongoing criminal conduct in violation of Code
  1 24 chapter 706A, a felony offense involving money laundering in
  1 25 violation of Code chapter 706B, or a felony offense involving
  1 26 human trafficking in violation of Code chapter 710A.   The bill
  1 27 also authorizes, upon application, the interception of wire,
  1 28 oral, or electronic communications if the court finds there
  1 29 is probable cause for belief that the evidence is related to
  1 30 a felony fugitive warrant issued in the state or involves an
  1 31 individual who is reasonably believed to be located within the
  1 32 state.
  1 33    Currently, the court may, upon application, enter an order
  1 34 authorizing the interception of wire, oral, or electronic
  1 35 communications if the court finds there is probable cause
  2  1 for belief that an individual is committing, has committed,
  2  2 or is about to commit a felony offense involving dealing in
  2  3 controlled substances, as defined in Code section 124.101.
