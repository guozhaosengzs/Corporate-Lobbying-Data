  1  3    1.  As used in this section:
  1  4    a.  "Agency" means the judicial branch, a state department or
  1  5 agency, the office of a county attorney, a criminal or juvenile
  1  6 justice agency, or a political subdivision of the state.
  1  7    b.  "Document" includes but is not limited to a copy of the
  1  8 following:
  1  9    (1)  Police reports.
  1 10    (2)  Photographs.
  1 11    (3)  Grand jury transcripts.
  1 12    (4)  Audiotapes.
  1 13    (5)  Videotapes.
  1 14    (6)  Transcripts of audiotapes or videotapes.
  1 15    (7)  Audio or video files on storage systems, including
  1 16 but not limited to disk, tape, optical, and other structured
  1 17 repositories for storing digital information.
  1 18    (8)  Transcripts of audio or video files on storage systems.
  1 19    (9)  Reports prepared by the department of human services.
  1 20    2.  An agency shall furnish copies of any document in the
  1 21 possession of the agency, at no cost, upon request of the state
  1 22 public defender, a public defender office, or an attorney
  1 23 or nonprofit legal organization appointed by the court as a
  1 24 designee of the state public defender, if the document is
  1 25 relevant to an investigation or legal representation being
  1 26 conducted by the state public defender, a public defender
  1 27 office, or an attorney or nonprofit legal organization
  1 28 appointed by the court as a designee of the state public
  1 29 defender. When a document is provided on a storage system, the
  1 30 agency shall, upon request, include the software necessary to
  1 31 access the storage system.
  1 32                           EXPLANATION
  1 33 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 34 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 35    This bill relates to the assessment of fees when a public
  2  1 defender or designee requests copies of certain documents.
  2  2    The bill defines "agency" to mean the judicial branch, a
  2  3 state department or agency, the office of a county attorney, a
  2  4 criminal or juvenile justice agency, or a political subdivision
  2  5 of the state.
  2  6    The bill requires that an agency furnish copies of any
  2  7 document in the possession of the agency, at no cost, upon
  2  8 request of the state public defender, a public defender office,
  2  9 or an attorney or nonprofit legal organization appointed by the
  2 10 court as a designee of the state public defender.
  2 11    The documents to be provided under the bill include but
  2 12 are not limited to copies of police reports, photographs,
  2 13 grand jury transcripts, audiotapes, videotapes, transcripts
  2 14 of audiotapes or videotapes, audio or video files on storage
  2 15 systems, transcripts of such audio or video files on storage
  2 16 systems, and reports prepared by the department of human
  2 17 services.
  2 18    The document requested must be relevant to an investigation
  2 19 or legal representation being conducted by the public defender
  2 20 or designee.
  2 21    Under the bill, when a document is provided on a storage
  2 22 system, the agency shall, upon request, include the software
  2 23 necessary to access the storage system.
