  1  1    Section 1.    1  1    Section 1.    1  2 occupants of land to trespassers.
  1  3    1.  A possessor of any fee, reversionary, or easement
  1  4 interest in real property, including but not limited to an
  1  5 owner, lessee, or other lawful occupant, owes no duty of care
  1  6 to a trespasser except to refrain from willfully or wantonly
  1  7 injuring the trespasser and to use reasonable care to avoid
  1  8 injuring the trespasser after that trespasser's presence
  1  9 becomes known.
  1 10    2.  This section shall not be construed to affect the common
  1 11 law doctrine of attractive nuisance.
  1 12    3.  This section does not create or increase the civil
  1 13 liability of any possessor or occupant of real property and
  1 14 does not affect any immunities from or defenses to civil
  1 15 liability established by another section of the Code or
  1 16 available at common law to which a possessor or occupant of
  1 17 real property may be entitled.
  1 18    Sec. 2.  APPLICABILITY.  This Act applies to all causes of
  1 19 action accrued on or after the effective date of this Act.

  1 21 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 22 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 23    This bill relates to the liability of a possessor or occupant
  1 24 of land for injury to a trespasser.
  1 25    The bill provides that a possessor of any fee, reversionary,
  1 26 or easement interest in real property, including but not
  1 27 limited to an owner, lessee, or other lawful occupant, owes no
  1 28 duty of care to a trespasser except to refrain from willfully
  1 29 or wantonly injuring the trespasser and to use reasonable
  1 30 care to avoid injuring the trespasser after that trespasser's
  1 31 presence becomes known.
  1 32    The bill does not affect the common law doctrine of
  1 33 attractive nuisance, which imposes a duty on a landowner to
  1 34 protect children from dangerous conditions of or items on
  1 35 the property that will foreseeably attract children onto the
  2  1 property.
  2  2    The bill does not create or increase the civil liability of
  2  3 any possessor or occupant of real property and does not affect
  2  4 any immunities from or defenses to civil liability established
  2  5 by another Code section or available at common law to which a
  2  6 possessor or occupant of real property may be entitled.
  2  7    The bill applies to all causes of action accrued on or after
  2  8 the effective date of the bill.
