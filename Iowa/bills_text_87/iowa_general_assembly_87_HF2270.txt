  1 15                           EXPLANATION
  1 16 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 17 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 18    This bill relates to kidnapping in the second degree. The
  1 19 criminal offense of kidnapping is defined in Code section
  1 20 710.1.
  1 21    Under the bill, a person commits kidnapping in the second
  1 22 degree when the victim is under 18 years of age other than a
  1 23 kidnapping by a parent or legal guardian whose sole purpose
  1 24 of the kidnapping is to assume custody of a victim under 18
  1 25 years of age.  Currently, a person commits kidnapping in the
  1 26 second degree when a person kidnaps another person and holds
  1 27 that person for ransom or when the kidnapper is armed with a
  1 28 dangerous weapon.
  1 29    A person who commits kidnapping in the second degree commits
  1 30 a class "B" forcible felony. A person who commits a forcible
  1 31 felony is ineligible to receive a deferred judgment or sentence
  1 32 or suspended sentence under Code section 907.3.
  1 33    A class "B" felony is punishable by confinement for no more
  1 34 than 25 years.  A person who commits kidnapping in the second
  1 35 degree accumulates reduced earned time under Code section
  2  1 903A.2 and must serve a minimum sentence of at least 70 percent
  2  2 of the sentence prior to being eligible for parole.
  2  3    A person who commits kidnapping in the second degree under
  2  4 the bill must also register as a tier III sex offender under
  2  5 Code chapter 692A if a determination is made that the offense
  2  6 was sexually motivated.
