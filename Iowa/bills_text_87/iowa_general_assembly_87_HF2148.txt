  2  6 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  2  7 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  2  8    Current law authorizes cities to prohibit or limit the use
  2  9 of consumer fireworks, display fireworks, or novelties by
  2 10 ordinance or resolution. A person who violates such ordinance
  2 11 or resolution commits a simple misdemeanor, punishable by a
  2 12 fine of at least $250.
  2 13    This bill permits cities to classify a violation of an
  2 14 ordinance or resolution prohibiting or limiting the use of
  2 15 consumer fireworks, display fireworks, or novelties as a
  2 16 municipal infraction. The bill provides that a person who uses
  2 17 such fireworks while the use of such devices is prohibited or
  2 18 limited by a local ordinance commits a simple misdemeanor,
  2 19 unless a city classifies a violation of such ordinance or
  2 20 resolution as a municipal infraction, whereby the person
  2 21 commits a municipal infraction in accordance with Code section
  2 22 364.22. A municipal infraction is a civil offense punishable
  2 23 by a civil penalty of not more than $750 for each violation or,
  2 24 if the infraction is a repeat offense, a civil penalty of not
  2 25 more than $1,000 for each repeat offense.
