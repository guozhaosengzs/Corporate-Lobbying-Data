  1 23                           EXPLANATION
  1 24 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 25 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 26    This bill relates to hearings for temporary protective
  1 27 orders in domestic abuse and sexual abuse cases.
  1 28    Under current law, a hearing for a temporary protective
  1 29 order in a domestic abuse case or sexual abuse case is required
  1 30 to be held not less than five and no more than 15 days after
  1 31 the filing of a petition for a domestic abuse or sexual abuse
  1 32 protective order.  The bill provides that if a hearing is not
  1 33 held within the time period specified, the petition shall
  1 34 be dismissed and the plaintiff and the respondent shall be
  1 35 notified.  The bill strikes provisions in current Code that
  2  1 provide that if a hearing in either case is continued by the
  2  2 court, the court may make or extend any temporary order that
  2  3 it deems necessary.
