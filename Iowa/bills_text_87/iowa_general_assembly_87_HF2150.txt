  1 10 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 11 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 12    This bill provides that a person commits a class "C" felony
  1 13 when the person unintentionally causes the death of another by
  1 14 exceeding a speed limit established or posted pursuant to Code
  1 15 section 321.236, subsection 13, paragraph "a" (powers of local
  1 16 authorities to regulate speed in rural residence districts),
  1 17 Code section 321.285 (general speed restrictions), Code
  1 18 section 321.288, subsection 2, paragraph "f" (reduced speed
  1 19 in road work zones), or Code section 321.289 (speed signs),
  1 20 321.290 (special restrictions), 321.293 (local authorities may
  1 21 alter limits), or 321.295 (limitation on bridge or elevated
  1 22 structures), by 25 miles per hour or more, if the death of the
  1 23 other person directly or indirectly results from the violation.
  1 24 A class "C" felony is punishable by imprisonment for no more
  1 25 than 10 years and a fine of at least $1,000 but not more than
  1 26 $10,000.
  1 27    By operation of law, a person who commits a violation
  1 28 under the bill is subject to driver's license suspension for
  1 29 vehicular homicide (Code section 321.210D) and is disqualified
  1 30 from operating a commercial motor vehicle for one year (Code
  1 31 section 321.208). In addition, the person is prohibited from
  1 32 being admitted to bail during the appeal process (Code section
  1 33 811.1). If the person fails to remain at the scene of the
  1 34 accident in violation of Code section 321.261, the person
  1 35 must also be denied parole or work release until the person
  2  1 has served at least seven=tenths of the maximum term of the
  2  2 person's sentence (Code section 902.12).
