 A BILL FOR

  1 An Act establishing a peer support respite house pilot program,
  2    and making an appropriation.
  3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF IOWA:
    TLSB 5120XS (3) 87
    hb/rh

PAG LIN



  1  1    Section 1.  PEER SUPPORT RESPITE HOUSE PILOT PROGRAM.
  1  2    1.  The department of human services shall establish,
  1  3 promote, and administer a two=year peer support respite house
  1  4 pilot program for the purposes of assisting persons with mental
  1  5 health and substance abuse disorders.  Financial assistance
  1  6 under the program shall be provided from moneys appropriated to
  1  7 the peer support respite house pilot program.
  1  8    2.  The department of human services shall award a grant
  1  9 to an entity headquartered in a county with a population
  1 10 between 48,000 and 51,000 as determined by the 2010 federal
  1 11 decennial census that provides multiple services to persons
  1 12 with substance abuse or mental health disorders, including
  1 13 but not limited to peer mentoring, short=term shelter, and
  1 14 referrals for mental health and substance abuse services.  The
  1 15 entity receiving the grant from the department shall have the
  1 16 demonstrated knowledge of evidence=based expertise in the
  1 17 field, proper training, a sound strategic plan, and documented
  1 18 control of a physical facility for the operation of a peer
  1 19 support respite house.
  1 20    3.  a.  The entity shall conduct a comprehensive review of
  1 21 its peer support respite house pilot program and shall, by
  1 22 December 15, 2020, submit a report of its review including any
  1 23 recommendations and cost projections to the governor and the
  1 24 general assembly.
  1 25    b.  The entity shall include in the report information
  1 26 relating to the establishment of peer support respite houses
  1 27 throughout the state.
  1 28    Sec. 2.  APPROPRIATION ==== PEER SUPPORT RESPITE HOUSE PILOT
  1 29 PROGRAM.  There is appropriated from the general fund of the
  1 30 state to the department of human services for the following
  1 31 fiscal years, the following amounts, or so much thereof as is
  1 32 necessary, to be used for the purposes designated:
  1 33    For the peer support respite house pilot program created in
  1 34 this Act:
  1 35    FY 2018=2019
  2  1 .................................................. $    150,000
  2  2    FY 2019=2020
  2  3 .................................................. $    150,000
  2  4    Notwithstanding section 8.33, moneys appropriated in this
  2  5 section of this Act that remain encumbered or unobligated at
  2  6 the close of the fiscal year shall not revert but shall remain
  2  7 available for expenditure for the purposes designated until the
  2  8 close of the succeeding fiscal year.
  2  9                           EXPLANATION
  2 10 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  2 11 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  2 12    This bill establishes a two=year peer support respite house
  2 13 pilot program for persons with mental health and substance
  2 14 abuse disorders and makes an appropriation.
  2 15    The bill directs the department of human services to
  2 16 establish and administer a two=year peer support respite house
  2 17 pilot program to provide services to persons with mental health
  2 18 and substance abuse disorder needs.  The department is required
  2 19 to award a grant to an entity located in a county with a
  2 20 population between 48,000 and 51,000 as determined by the 2010
  2 21 federal decennial census that provides multiple services to
  2 22 persons with substance abuse or mental health disorders. The
  2 23 entity receiving the grant shall have demonstrated knowledge of
  2 24 evidence=based expertise, proper training, a solid strategic
  2 25 plan, and documented control of a physical facility to be used
  2 26 for the operation of a peer support respite house.
  2 27    The bill requires the entity to conduct a comprehensive
  2 28 review and submit a report with recommendations and cost
  2 29 projections by December 15, 2020, to the governor and the
  2 30 general assembly.  The report shall include the feasibility of
  2 31 establishing peer support respite houses throughout the state.
  2 32    The bill appropriates $150,000 from the general fund of the
  2 33 state to the department of human services for FY 2018=2019 and
  2 34 FY 2019=2020 for purposes of the bill.
