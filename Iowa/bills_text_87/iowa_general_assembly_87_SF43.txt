  1  1    Section 1.    1  1    Section 1.    1  2 assistance for drug=related overdose.
  1  3    1.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise
  1  4 requires:
  1  5    a.  "Drug=related overdose" means a condition of a person for
  1  6 which each of the following is true:
  1  7    (1)  The person is in need of medical assistance.
  1  8    (2)  The person displays symptoms including but not limited
  1  9 to extreme physical illness, pinpoint pupils, decreased level
  1 10 of consciousness including coma, or respiratory depression.
  1 11    (3)  The person's condition is the result of, or a prudent
  1 12 layperson would reasonably believe such condition to be the
  1 13 result of, the consumption or use of a controlled substance.
  1 14    b.  "Overdose patient" means a person who is, or would
  1 15 reasonably be perceived to be, suffering a drug=related
  1 16 overdose.
  1 17    c.  "Overdose reporter" means a person who seeks medical
  1 18 assistance for an overdose patient.
  1 19    d.  "Protected information" means information or evidence
  1 20 collected or derived as a result of any of the following:
  1 21    (1)  An overdose patient's good=faith actions to seek
  1 22 medical assistance while experiencing a drug=related overdose.
  1 23    (2)  An overdose reporter's good=faith actions to seek
  1 24 medical assistance for an overdose patient experiencing a
  1 25 drug=related overdose if all of the following are true:
  1 26    (a)  The overdose patient is in need of medical assistance
  1 27 for an immediate health or safety concern.
  1 28    (b)  The overdose reporter is the first person to seek
  1 29 medical assistance for the overdose patient.
  1 30    (c)  The overdose reporter provides the overdose reporter's
  1 31 name and contact information to medical or law enforcement
  1 32 personnel.
  1 33    (d)  The overdose reporter remains on the scene until
  1 34 assistance arrives or is provided.
  1 35    (e)  The overdose reporter cooperates with medical and law
  2  1 enforcement personnel.
  2  2    2.  Protected information shall not be considered to support
  2  3 probable cause and shall not be admissible as evidence against
  2  4 an overdose patient or overdose reporter for any of the
  2  5 following offenses:
  2  6    a.  Violation of section 124.401, subsection 1.
  2  7    b.  Possession of a controlled substance under section
  2  8 124.401, subsection 5.
  2  9    c.  Violation of section 124.407.
  2 10    d.  Violation of section 124.414.
  2 11    3.  A person's pretrial release, probation, supervised
  2 12 release, or parole shall not be revoked based on protected
  2 13 information.
  2 14    4.  Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the
  2 15 contrary, a court may consider the act of providing first aid
  2 16 or other medical assistance to someone who is experiencing a
  2 17 drug=related overdose as a mitigating factor in a criminal
  2 18 prosecution.
  2 19    5.  This section shall not be construed to limit the use or
  2 20 admissibility of any evidence in a criminal case other than as
  2 21 provided in subsection 2.
  2 22                           EXPLANATION
  2 23 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  2 24 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  2 25    This bill provides immunity from certain criminal offenses
  2 26 when a person reports or seeks medical assistance for a
  2 27 drug=related overdose.
  2 28    Under the bill, if a person seeks medical assistance for
  2 29 another person experiencing a drug=related overdose, or if
  2 30 a person experiencing a drug=related overdose seeks medical
  2 31 assistance, the person is immune from prosecution for the
  2 32 possession or use of a controlled substance or possession of
  2 33 drug paraphernalia.  Immunity for a person who seeks medical
  2 34 assistance for another is only available if the person is
  2 35 the first to seek medical assistance, provides such person's
  3  1 name and contact information to medical or law enforcement
  3  2 personnel, remains on the scene until assistance arrives or
  3  3 is provided, and cooperates with medical and law enforcement
  3  4 personnel.
  3  5    The bill further provides that a person's pretrial release,
  3  6 probation, supervised release, or parole shall not be revoked
  3  7 based on information collected because the person, personally
  3  8 or for another, sought medical assistance due to a drug=related
  3  9 overdose.
  3 10    The bill provides that a court may consider the act of
  3 11 providing first aid or other medical assistance to someone who
  3 12 is experiencing a drug=related overdose as a mitigating factor
  3 13 in a criminal prosecution.
