  1 26                           EXPLANATION
  1 27 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 28 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 29    This bill relates to the criminal offense of manufacturing,
  1 30 delivering, selling, or possessing drug paraphernalia and the
  1 31 establishment of a syringe exchange program.
  1 32    Currently, a person who illegally manufactures, delivers,
  1 33 sells, or possesses drug paraphernalia commits a simple
  1 34 misdemeanor under Code section 124.414.  Current law also
  1 35 provides that "drug paraphernalia" does not include hypodermic
  2  1 needles or syringes if manufactured, delivered, sold, or
  2  2 possessed for a lawful purpose. The bill specifies that
  2  3 "lawful purpose" includes hypodermic needles or syringes
  2  4 delivered, sold, or possessed through an approved syringe
  2  5 services exchange program established pursuant to rules adopted
  2  6 by the department of public health.
  2  7    The bill provides that the Iowa department of public health
  2  8 shall establish and implement a syringe services program for
  2  9 persons who unlawfully inject controlled substances and who are
  2 10 at an increased risk for exposure to hepatitis B or hepatitis
  2 11 C.  The program shall include hepatitis C testing, referrals
  2 12 for treatment of substance use disorders, and training for
  2 13 opioid overdose prevention and reversal.
  2 14    The bill provides that the Iowa department of public health
  2 15 shall also establish and implement a syringe services program
  2 16 for people who unlawfully inject controlled substances and
  2 17 who are at risk of HIV infection.  The program shall include
  2 18 testing for HIV infection, referrals for treatment of substance
  2 19 use disorders, and training in opioid overdose prevention and
  2 20 reversal.
