 SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION

  1 A Joint Resolution proposing an amendment to the Constitution
  2    of the State of Iowa relating to public retirement systems.
  3 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF IOWA:
    TLSB 5816XS (3) 87
    ec/rn

PAG LIN



  1  1    Section 1.  The following amendment to the Constitution of
  1  2 the State of Iowa is proposed:
  1  3    The Constitution of the State of Iowa is amended by adding
  1  4 the following new section to new Article XIII:
  1  5    ARTICLE XIII.
  1  6    TWO=THIRDS MAJORITY FOR PUBLIC RETIREMENT SYSTEM LAW CHANGES.
  1  7 Two=thirds majority to change public retirement system laws.
  1  8    SECTION 1.  A bill containing provisions enacting, amending,
  1  9 or repealing any provision relating to a public retirement
  1 10 system shall require the affirmative votes of at least
  1 11 two=thirds of the whole membership of each house of the general
  1 12 assembly for passage.
  1 13    Sec. 2.  REFERRAL AND PUBLICATION.  The foregoing proposed
  1 14 amendment to the Constitution of the State of Iowa is referred
  1 15 to the general assembly to be chosen at the next general
  1 16 election for members of the general assembly, and the secretary
  1 17 of state is directed to cause the proposed amendment to be
  1 18 published for three consecutive months previous to the date of
  1 19 that election as provided by law.
  1 20                           EXPLANATION
  1 21 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 22 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 23    This joint resolution proposes an amendment to the
  1 24 Constitution of the State of Iowa relating to public retirement
  1 25 systems.  The resolution requires that any bill containing
  1 26 provisions enacting, amending, or repealing any provision
  1 27 relating to a public retirement system shall require the
  1 28 affirmative votes of at least two=thirds of the whole
  1 29 membership of each house of the general assembly for passage.
  1 30    The resolution, if adopted, would be published and then
  1 31 referred to the next general assembly (88th) for adoption,
  1 32 before being submitted to the electorate for ratification.
