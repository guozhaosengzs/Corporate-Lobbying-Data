  1  3    1.  For the purposes of this chapter:
  1  4    a.  "Abortion" means as defined in section 146.1.
  1  5    b.  "Health care facility" means as defined in section
  1  6 135C.1.
  1  7    c.  "Public funds" means state, county, or local government
  1  8 moneys authorized or appropriated pursuant to state law or
  1  9 derived from any fund in which such moneys are deposited.
  1 10 "Public funds" does not include federal moneys received by the
  1 11 state under Title X of the federal Public Health Service Act
  1 12 as specified in 42 U.S.C. {300 et seq. for family planning
  1 13 services; the personal responsibility education program
  1 14 as specified in 42 U.S.C. {713; the separate program for
  1 15 abstinence education as specified in 42 U.S.C. {710; or through
  1 16 the temporary assistance for needy families program for the
  1 17 community adolescent pregnancy prevention program.
  1 18    2.  Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the
  1 19 contrary, public funds shall not be expended for the purpose
  1 20 of providing abortions.
  1 21    3.  Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the
  1 22 contrary, health care services furnished by or in a health
  1 23 care facility owned or operated by the state or a county or
  1 24 local government entity or by a physician or other individual
  1 25 employed by the state or a county or local government entity
  1 26 to provide health care services within the scope of practice
  1 27 and employment of the individual shall not include provision
  1 28 of abortions.
  1 29    4.  Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the
  1 30 contrary, any amount paid by an individual or an entity during
  1 31 a taxable year for an abortion or as premiums for health care
  1 32 coverage that includes coverage for abortions is not tax
  1 33 deductible under chapter 422.
  1 34    5.  This chapter shall not be construed to apply to any of
  1 35 the following:
  2  1    a.  The treatment of any infection, injury, disease,
  2  2 or disorder that has been caused by or exacerbated by the
  2  3 performance of an abortion, whether the abortion was performed
  2  4 in accordance with state and federal law or whether funding for
  2  5 the abortion is permissible under this section.
  2  6    b.  An abortion when the woman suffers from a physical
  2  7 disorder, physical injury, or physical illness that would, as
  2  8 certified by a physician, place the woman in danger of death
  2  9 unless an abortion is performed, including a life=endangering
  2 10 physical condition caused by or arising from the pregnancy
  2 11 itself.
  2 12    c.  The treatment of a woman for a spontaneous abortion,
  2 13 commonly known as a miscarriage.
  2 14                           EXPLANATION
  2 15 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  2 16 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  2 17    This bill creates a new Code chapter 146B, providing
  2 18 restrictions on the expenditure of public funds for abortions
  2 19 notwithstanding any other provisions of law to the contrary.
  2 20    The bill defines "abortion", "health care facility", and
  2 21 "public funds" for the purposes of the new Code chapter.
  2 22    Public funds are prohibited from being expended for the
  2 23 purpose of providing abortions.
  2 24    The new Code chapter prohibits health care services
  2 25 furnished by or in a health care facility owned or operated
  2 26 by the state or a county or local government entity or by a
  2 27 physician or other individual employed by the state or a county
  2 28 or local government entity to provide health care services
  2 29 within the scope of practice and employment of the individual
  2 30 from including the provision of abortions.
  2 31    Any amount paid by an individual or an entity during a
  2 32 taxable year for an abortion or as premiums for health care
  2 33 coverage that includes coverage for abortions is not tax
  2 34 deductible under Code chapter 422.
  2 35    The new Code chapter is not to be construed to apply to
  3  1 the treatment of any infection, injury, disease, or disorder
  3  2 that has been caused by or exacerbated by the performance of
  3  3 an abortion, whether the abortion was performed in accordance
  3  4 with state and federal law or whether funding for the abortion
  3  5 is permissible under the Code chapter; an abortion when the
  3  6 woman suffers from a physical disorder, physical injury, or
  3  7 physical illness that would, as certified by a physician, place
  3  8 the woman in danger of death unless an abortion is performed,
  3  9 including a life=endangering physical condition caused by or
  3 10 arising from the pregnancy itself; or the treatment of a woman
  3 11 for a miscarriage.
