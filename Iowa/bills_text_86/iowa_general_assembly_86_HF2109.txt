  2 10                           EXPLANATION
  2 11 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  2 12 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  2 13    This bill requires the annual certification of automated
  2 14 traffic law enforcement (ATE) systems by the department of
  2 15 transportation (DOT).  The bill provides that the DOT shall
  2 16 adopt rules regarding the calibration, certification, and
  2 17 operation of ATE systems and their components, including but
  2 18 not limited to rules in accordance with the speed enforcement
  2 19 camera systems operational guidelines and speed measuring
  2 20 device performance specifications set forth by the national
  2 21 highway and traffic safety administration. The bill prohibits
  2 22 the certification of an ATE system by its manufacturer or
  2 23 operator, unless the operator is the DOT.
  2 24    The bill requires all fines collected from the use of an ATE
  2 25 system to be deposited with the clerk of the district court in
  2 26 the county where the ATE system is located for distribution
  2 27 under Code section 602.8106 (collection of fees in criminal
  2 28 cases and disposition of fees and fines).
  2 29    The bill prohibits a private entity that maintains,
  2 30 operates, or otherwise administers an ATE system from receiving
  2 31 the personal information of a person issued a citation as a
  2 32 result of the use of the ATE system.
