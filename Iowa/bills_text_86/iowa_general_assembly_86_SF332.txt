  1  3    1.  The department may only designate an individual as
  1  4 ineligible to apply for; to be considered, referred, or
  1  5 approved for; or to be appointed to employment by a state
  1  6 agency for any of the following reasons:
  1  7    a.  The individual has knowingly misrepresented the facts
  1  8 when submitting information relating to an application,
  1  9 examination, certification, appeal, or any other facet of the
  1 10 selection process for employment by a state agency.
  1 11    b.  The individual has used or attempted to use coercion,
  1 12 bribery, or other illegal means to secure an advantage in the
  1 13 application, examination, appeal, or selection process for
  1 14 employment by a state agency.
  1 15    c.  The individual has been convicted of a crime that is
  1 16 shown to have a direct relationship to the duties of the
  1 17 job class or position for which the individual wishes to be
  1 18 considered.
  1 19    d.  The individual is proven to be an unrehabilitated
  1 20 substance abuser who would be unable to perform the duties of
  1 21 the job class or position for which the individual wishes to be
  1 22 considered or who would constitute a threat to state property
  1 23 or to the safety of others.
  1 24    e.  The individual is not a United States citizen and
  1 25 does not have a valid permit to work in the United States
  1 26 under regulations issued by the United States citizenship and
  1 27 immigration services.
  1 28    f.  The individual was terminated from employment by a state
  1 29 agency for creating a hostile work environment.
  1 30    2.  The department shall do all of the following upon a
  1 31 determination that an individual is ineligible as provided in
  1 32 subsection 1:
  1 33    a.  Notify the individual in writing within five working
  1 34 days following the designation of ineligibility and the extent
  1 35 of the individual's ineligibility for employment by a state
  2  1 agency. The notification shall include information on the
  2  2 process for an individual to appeal, remove, or modify the
  2  3 designation of ineligibility.
  2  4    b.  Maintain documentation of the designation of
  2  5 ineligibility, the extent of the individual's ineligibility
  2  6 for employment by a state agency, proof of notification of
  2  7 the individual, and any information concerning any appeals
  2  8 regarding the designation.
  2  9    c.  Upon request by an individual determined to be
  2 10 ineligible, review whether the designation of ineligibility of
  2 11 the individual should be removed or modified and to notify the
  2 12 individual, in writing, if the designation of ineligibility is
  2 13 removed or modified.
  2 14    3.  An individual designated as ineligible as provided
  2 15 by this section may file a written appeal to the employment
  2 16 appeal board in the department of inspections and appeals for a
  2 17 review of that action.  The written appeal must be filed with
  2 18 the board within sixty days following the notice of initial
  2 19 designation of ineligibility.  The department shall also
  2 20 establish a procedure for an individual, following the time for
  2 21 filing an appeal, to apply to the department for removal or
  2 22 modification of a designation of ineligibility.
  2 23    4.  The department shall adopt rules pursuant to chapter 17A
  2 24 to administer this section.
