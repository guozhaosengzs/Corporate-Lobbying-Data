  1  1    Section 1.  
  1  1    Section 1.    1  1    Section 1.    1  2    1.  A wireless communications service provider shall
  1  3 provide call location information concerning a device to a
  1  4 law enforcement agency or officer upon a request for that
  1  5 information if the law enforcement agency determines the
  1  6 information is needed in an emergency situation that involves
  1  7 the risk of death or serious physical harm.
  1  8    2.  Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary,
  1  9 nothing in this section prohibits a wireless communications
  1 10 service provider from establishing protocols by which the
  1 11 provider could voluntarily disclose call location information
  1 12 to a law enforcement agency or officer upon a request for that
  1 13 information.
  1 14    3.  A claim or cause of action may not be brought against
  1 15 any wireless communications service provider or employee for
  1 16 providing call location information while acting in good faith
  1 17 and in accordance with the provisions of this section.
  1 18    4.  a.  Wireless communications service providers authorized
  1 19 to do business in the state of Iowa, or submitting to
  1 20 the jurisdiction of Iowa, and all resellers of wireless
  1 21 communications services authorized to do business in the
  1 22 state shall submit contact information to the department of
  1 23 public safety, division of criminal investigation, in order to
  1 24 facilitate requests from law enforcement agencies pursuant to
  1 25 this section.  Wireless communications service providers and
  1 26 resellers of wireless communications services shall submit this
  1 27 contact information annually by June 15 or immediately upon any
  1 28 change in contact information.
  1 29    b.  The division of criminal investigation shall maintain
  1 30 a database containing emergency contact information for all
  1 31 wireless communications service providers and all resellers of
  1 32 wireless communications services authorized to do business in
  1 33 the state and shall make the information immediately available
  1 34 upon request to all public safety answering points in the
  1 35 state.
  2  1    5.  A person filing a false report with law enforcement that
  2  2 results in a request for call location information under this
  2  3 section may be subject to criminal penalty pursuant to section
  2  4 718.6.
  2  5    Sec. 2.  RULES.  The division of criminal investigation
  2  6 shall, by December 31, 2015, adopt rules to implement and
  2  7 administer this Act.
  2  8    Sec. 3.  EFFECTIVE DATE.  The section of this Act enacting
  2  9 section 34A.16 takes effect January 1, 2016.
  2 10                           EXPLANATION
  2 11 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  2 12 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  2 13    This bill enacts new Code section 34A.16 requiring wireless
  2 14 communications service providers to provide call location
  2 15 information concerning a device upon the request of a law
  2 16 enforcement agency or officer.  The agency or officer may
  2 17 request the call location information for emergency situations
  2 18 involving the risk of death or serious harm.  The bill states
  2 19 that a claim or cause of action may not be brought against
  2 20 wireless communications service providers or employees when
  2 21 acting in good faith in accordance with the bill.  The bill
  2 22 also requires wireless communications service providers and
  2 23 resellers to provide contact information to the division of
  2 24 criminal investigation of the department of public safety.  The
  2 25 bill provides that the division of criminal investigation is
  2 26 required to maintain a database containing this information
  2 27 and the information is to be made immediately available upon
  2 28 request to all public safety answering points in the state.
  2 29 New Code section 34A.16 takes effect January 1, 2016.
