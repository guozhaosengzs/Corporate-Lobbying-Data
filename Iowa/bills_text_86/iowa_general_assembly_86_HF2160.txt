  1  1    Section 1.    1  1    Section 1.    1  2    1.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise
  1  3 requires:
  1  4    a.  "Baited area" means any area where feed is placed,
  1  5 exposed, deposited, distributed, or scattered with the intent
  1  6 to lure, attract, or entice deer to a specific location.
  1  7    b.  "Feed" means grain, fruit, vegetables, nuts, hay, salt,
  1  8 mineral blocks, or any other natural food materials; commercial
  1  9 products containing natural food materials; or by=products of
  1 10 such materials, that are capable of luring, attracting, or
  1 11 enticing deer to a specific location. "Feed" does not include
  1 12 food placed during normal agricultural activities including but
  1 13 not limited to feed placed for livestock that are present and
  1 14 are actively consuming the feed on a regular basis.
  1 15    2.  A person shall not hunt, take, or attempt to take deer
  1 16 on or in a baited area.
  1 17    3.  A person shall not place, expose, deposit, distribute,
  1 18 or scatter feed on a baited area on any private or public
  1 19 property, or knowingly allow another person to place, expose,
  1 20 deposit, distribute, or scatter feed on a baited area on
  1 21 private property under the person's ownership or lease in
  1 22 the state unless all feed is removed from the baited area at
  1 23 least ten days prior to the opening day of the first fall deer
  1 24 hunting season. An area remains a baited area for ten days
  1 25 following complete removal of all feed from the area or until
  1 26 the area is covered in such a manner that the area no longer
  1 27 serves to artificially attract deer. Land being used to hunt,
  1 28 take, or attempt to take deer that is adjacent to a baited area
  1 29 shall not be considered a baited area if the baited area is
  1 30 on private property that is not under the ownership, lease,
  1 31 or control of the owner of the adjacent land or of the person
  1 32 hunting on the adjacent land.

  2  3                           EXPLANATION
  2  4 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  2  5 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  2  6    This bill prohibits hunting, taking, or attempting to take
  2  7 deer on or in a baited area. The bill also requires that if a
  2  8 person feeds deer in a baited area, all feed must be removed
  2  9 from that area at least 10 days before the opening day of the
  2 10 first fall deer hunting season. An area ceases to be a baited
  2 11 area 10 days following complete removal of all feed from the
  2 12 area or when the area is covered in such a manner that the area
  2 13 no longer serves to artificially attract deer. Land being used
  2 14 to hunt, take, or attempt to take deer that is adjacent to a
  2 15 baited area shall not be considered a baited area if the baited
  2 16 area is on private property that is not under the ownership,
  2 17 lease, or control of the owner of the adjacent land or of the
  2 18 person hunting on the adjacent land.
  2 19    For purposes of the bill, "feed" is grain, fruit,
  2 20 vegetables, nuts, hay, salt, mineral blocks, or any other
  2 21 natural food materials; commercial products containing
  2 22 natural food materials; or by=products of such materials,
  2 23 that are capable of luring, attracting, or enticing deer to a
  2 24 specific location. "Feed" does not include food placed during
  2 25 normal agricultural activities including but not limited to
  2 26 feed placed for livestock that are present and are actively
  2 27 consuming the feed on a regular basis. A "baited area" is any
  2 28 area where feed is placed, exposed, deposited, distributed, or
  2 29 scattered with the intent to lure, attract, or entice deer to a
  2 30 specific location.
  2 31    A violation of the bill's provisions is punishable with a
  2 32 scheduled fine of $100.
