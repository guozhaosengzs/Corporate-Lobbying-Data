  1 34 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 35 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  2  1 Current law permits a state agency, upon request and
  2  2 approval by the department of administrative services, to make
  2  3 a purchase of goods or services directly from a vendor, rather
  2  4 than purchasing through the department, if one of certain
  2  5 conditions is met. One of the conditions is if the purchase
  2  6 will not exceed $10,000 and the purchase would contribute to
  2  7 the agency complying with targeted small business procurement
  2  8 goals.
  2  9    This bill modifies that condition to permit a state agency
  2 10 to engage in direct purchasing for any purchase that will
  2 11 not exceed $10,000. The bill strikes the requirement for
  2 12 a prior request and approval by the department for direct
  2 13 purchasing. The bill requires a state agency that makes such
  2 14 a purchase to promptly notify the department that it has done
  2 15 so. The bill provides that the notification must include
  2 16 documentation demonstrating that the purchase met the criteria
  2 17 for a direct purchase provided in the bill. The bill provides
  2 18 that prior notification of or approval by the department for
  2 19 such purchases shall not be required. The bill provides that
  2 20 competitive bidding shall not be required for such purchases.
  2 21    The bill also provides that a state agency that meets the
  2 22 requirements for direct purchasing provided in the bill may
  2 23 engage in direct purchasing instead of purchasing pursuant to a
  2 24 master contract established by the department.
