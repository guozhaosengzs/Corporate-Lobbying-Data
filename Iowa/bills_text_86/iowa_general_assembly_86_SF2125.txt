 A BILL FOR

  1 An Act relating to the termination of Medicaid managed care
  2    contracts, and including effective date provisions.
  3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF IOWA:
    TLSB 5965SV (1) 86
    pf/nh

PAG LIN



  1  1    Section 1.  TERMINATION OF MEDICAID MANAGED CARE CONTRACTS
  1  2 == ALTERNATIVES FOR HEALTH CARE DELIVERY AND PAYMENT REFORM.
  1  3    1.  The department of human services shall, upon the
  1  4 effective date of this Act, provide thirty days' written notice
  1  5 in accordance with the termination provisions of the contract,
  1  6 to each managed care organization with whom the department
  1  7 executed a contract to administer the Iowa high quality health
  1  8 care initiative, to terminate all such contracts.
  1  9    2.  The department shall continue to pursue other
  1 10 initiatives to realign the health care delivery system and
  1 11 provide holistic, integrated, patient=centered care while
  1 12 moving toward a value=based model of payment reform, including
  1 13 but not limited to the healthiest state initiative, the Iowa
  1 14 health and wellness plan created pursuant to chapter 249N, the
  1 15 state innovation models initiative utilizing accountable care
  1 16 organizations, and integrated health homes.
  1 17    Sec. 2.  EFFECTIVE UPON ENACTMENT.  This Act, being deemed of
  1 18 immediate importance, takes effect upon enactment.
  1 19                           EXPLANATION
  1 20 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 21 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 22    This bill directs the department of human services to
  1 23 provide 30 days' written notice, upon the effective date of
  1 24 the Act, to terminate the contracts with each managed care
  1 25 organization with whom the department executed a contract to
  1 26 administer the Iowa high quality health care initiative.
  1 27    The bill directs the department to continue to pursue other
  1 28 initiatives to realign the health care delivery system and
  1 29 provide holistic, integrated, patient=centered care while
  1 30 moving toward a value=based model of payment, including but not
  1 31 limited to the healthiest state initiative, the Iowa health
  1 32 and wellness plan created pursuant to Code chapter 249N, the
  1 33 state innovation models initiative utilizing accountable care
  1 34 organizations, and integrated health homes.
  1 35    The bill takes effect upon enactment.
