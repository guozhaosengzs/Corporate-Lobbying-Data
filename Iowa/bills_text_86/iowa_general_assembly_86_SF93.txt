  5  1 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  5  2 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  5  3    This bill provides for an adjustment in the assessed value
  5  4 of a homestead, as defined in the bill, if the owner is a
  5  5 person who is 65 or older. The bill also limits the adjustment
  5  6 in assessed value to homesteads that have certain assessed
  5  7 valuations of less than $150,000. If those qualifications are
  5  8 met, the assessed value of the homestead upon which property
  5  9 taxes are levied in a fiscal year is the same assessed value as
  5 10 for the previous fiscal year.  The bill specifies that assessed
  5 11 value is that value prior to any rollback being applied.
  5 12    The bill provides that a person who makes a false affidavit
  5 13 for the purpose of obtaining an adjustment, knowingly receives
  5 14 the adjustment without being legally entitled to it, or makes
  5 15 claim for the adjustment in more than one county without being
  5 16 legally entitled to it is guilty of a fraudulent practice and
  5 17 is subject to a criminal penalty.
  5 18    The bill applies retroactively to January 1, 2015, for
  5 19 assessment years beginning on or after that date and applies to
  5 20 claims filed on or after January 1, 2016, for the adjustments.
