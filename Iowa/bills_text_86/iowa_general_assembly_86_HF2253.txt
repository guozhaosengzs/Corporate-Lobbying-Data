  6 11 food banks and emergency feeding organizations, both of which
  6 12 are involved in distributing food to low=income families and
  6 13 individuals and unemployed families and individuals in this
  6 14 state.  The bill establishes three distribution programs,
  6 15 two within the department of human services and including
  6 16 appropriations, and one within the Iowa department of
  6 17 corrections; and provides for a sales and use tax exemption.
  6 18    PROGRAMS AND APPROPRIATIONS.  An Iowa food bank association
  6 19 which is an organization with its principal place of business
  6 20 located in this state and comprised of food bank members, is
  6 21 to manage two programs under a contract entered into with
  6 22 the department of human services, which must oversee the
  6 23 programs. The programs allow the association to purchase
  6 24 or otherwise acquire: eligible agricultural products which
  6 25 include both (1) food and (2) agricultural commodities that
  6 26 can be processed into food.  The program also provides for the
  6 27 packaging, storing, and transporting of eligible agricultural
  6 28 products to nonprofit organizations for distribution.  The
  6 29 Iowa agricultural products clearance program is established to
  6 30 distribute eligible agricultural products to both food banks
  6 31 and emergency feeding organizations.  The Iowa emergency food
  6 32 purchase program is established to distribute food only to
  6 33 Iowa emergency feeding organizations.  The bill appropriates
  6 34 moneys from the general fund to the department to support
  6 35 administration of the two programs and the contract.
