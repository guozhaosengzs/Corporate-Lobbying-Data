 12 33                           EXPLANATION
 12 34 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
 12 35 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
 13  1 This bill relates to food and consumer safety by changing
 13  2 requirements for the hotel sanitation, home food establishment,
 13  3 food establishment and food processing plants Code chapters.
 13  4    Division I of the bill changes the information the local
 13  5 board of health responsible for enforcing the Iowa hotel
 13  6 sanitation code is required to provide to the director of
 13  7 the department of inspections and appeals regarding the
 13  8 hotel licenses granted in the year.  The division requires a
 13  9 regulatory authority, meaning the department of inspections and
 13 10 appeals or a local board of health, in conducting inspections
 13 11 of hotels to do so in accordance with rules adopted by the
 13 12 department of inspections and appeals.  The division provides
 13 13 that licensees may be subject to a penalty of 10 percent of
 13 14 the license fee per month rather than a flat 10 percent upon
 13 15 failure to renew a license on or before the expiration date.
 13 16 The division also changes the license fee schedule for hotel
 13 17 licenses.  The division provides that if anyone, rather than
 13 18 just a guest of a hotel, files a complaint that the premises
 13 19 is unsanitary, the regulatory authority must conduct an
 13 20 inspection.
 13 21    Division II of the bill changes the term "home food
 13 22 establishment" to "home bakery".  The division provides that
 13 23 the term "prepared food" for purposes of the home bakeries
 13 24 Code chapter means baked goods that are time and temperature
 13 25 controlled for food safety rather than hazardous baked goods.
 13 26 The division increases the fee for a home bakery license from
 13 27 $33.75 to $50.
 13 28    Division III of the bill relates to food establishments
 13 29 and food processing plants.  The division eliminates a food
 13 30 service operation in the Iowa juvenile home from the definition
 13 31 of food establishment.  The division adds a definition for
 13 32 "time/temperature control for safety food" and replaces
 13 33 references to potentially hazardous food with this phrase.
 13 34 The division defines the term "event" as a significant
 13 35 occurrence or happening sponsored by a civic, business,
 14  1 educational, governmental, community, or veterans organization
 14  2 and may include athletic contests.  The division modifies
 14  3 the definitions of "vending machine" and "vending machine
 14  4 location".
 14  5    The division eliminates the annual report a municipal
 14  6 corporation responsible for enforcing the food establishments
 14  7 and food processing plants Code chapter must provide the
 14  8 director of inspections and appeals, but requires the municipal
 14  9 corporation to use the data system prescribed by the director.
 14 10    The division requires a food establishment or food
 14 11 processing plant seeking licensure under the Code chapter to
 14 12 renew prior to the time of the expiration of licensure or
 14 13 within 60 days of the expiration date.
 14 14    The division adds a $250 general application fee
 14 15 for licensure under the Code chapter.  This general
 14 16 application fee does not apply to farmers markets, temporary
 14 17 food establishments for a single event, temporary food
 14 18 establishments for multiple nonconcurrent events, or vending
 14 19 machines.  However, the division requires a person conducting
 14 20 an event to submit an application and a $50 application fee
 14 21 to the appropriate regulatory authority 60 days prior to the
 14 22 event.  For purposes of this application and application fee,
 14 23 an "event" does not include a fair as defined in Code section
 14 24 174.1 or a farmers market with 10 or more temporary food
 14 25 establishments.
 14 26    The division changes the license fee schedule for mobile
 14 27 food units or pushcarts, temporary food establishments
 14 28 per fixed location for a single event, temporary food
 14 29 establishments for multiple nonconcurrent events, vending
 14 30 machines, food establishments which prepare or serve food for
 14 31 individual portion service intended for consumption on the
 14 32 premises, food establishments which sell food or food products
 14 33 intended for consumption off the premises, food processing
 14 34 plants, and farmers markets.  The division adds new categories
 14 35 of license fees for schools with production kitchens or serving
 15  1 sites and for certificates of free sale or sanitation.
 15  2    The division provides that the regulatory authority
 15  3 must document violations during regular inspections as it
 15  4 deems appropriate.  The division also states that a food
 15  5 establishment or food processing plant being inspected shall
 15  6 provide the regulatory authority access to records necessary to
 15  7 determine compliance and the regulatory authority may take food
 15  8 or environmental samples.
 15  9    The division provides that if any person, rather than
 15 10 a customer, files a complaint that the premises of a food
 15 11 establishment or food processing plant are not in compliance
 15 12 with the food establishments and food processing plants Code
 15 13 chapter, the regulatory authority may conduct an inspection.
 15 14 The bill allows the person filing the complaint to keep their
 15 15 name confidential.
 15 16    The division repeals the Code section subjecting persons in
 15 17 violation of the food establishments and food processing plants
 15 18 Code chapter to a $100 penalty.
