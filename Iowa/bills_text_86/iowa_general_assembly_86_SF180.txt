  1 16 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 17 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 18    This bill relates to the refusal to submit to a chemical test
  1 19 of blood in operating=while=intoxicated (OWI) cases.
  1 20    Current law provides that a person's refusal to submit to
  1 21 a chemical test of breath or urine in an OWI case constitutes
  1 22 grounds for the department of transportation (DOT) to
  1 23 administratively revoke the person's driver's license under
  1 24 Code section 321J.9. The bill expands the grounds sufficient
  1 25 for the DOT to revoke the person's driver's license under Code
  1 26 section 321J.9 to include a person's refusal to submit to a
  1 27 chemical test of blood.
