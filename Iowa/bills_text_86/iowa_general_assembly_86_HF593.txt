  1  1    Section 1.  
  1  1    Section 1.    1  1    Section 1.    1  2 station investment tax credit.
  1  3    1.  For purposes of this section, unless the context
  1  4 otherwise requires:
  1  5    a.  "Equity investment" means an equity interest in a
  1  6 business which equity interest was received in exchange for
  1  7 a capital contribution or payment in the form of cash, real
  1  8 property, or tangible personal property.
  1  9    b.  "Governing documents" means the organizing documents
  1 10 of a business including but not limited to a certificate of
  1 11 organization, certificate of limited partnership, articles of
  1 12 incorporation, or other similar document, and the operating
  1 13 documents of a business including but not limited to a
  1 14 partnership agreement, operating agreement, bylaws, or other
  1 15 similar documents.
  1 16    c.  "Qualified motor fuel pump station" means a business that
  1 17 meets all of the following requirements:
  1 18    (1)  The business is operated for profit and primarily
  1 19 engaged in the retail sale of motor fuel to consumers in this
  1 20 state.
  1 21    (2)  The business has a commercial domicile, as defined in
  1 22 section 422.32, in this state.
  1 23    (3)  The governing documents of the business require that
  1 24 at least one percent of the annual profits of the business be
  1 25 donated for the benefit of fire fighters, police officers, or
  1 26 emergency medical service personnel serving in this state, or
  1 27 veterans living in this state.
  1 28    2.  The taxes imposed under this division, less the credits
  1 29 allowed under section 422.12, shall be reduced by a qualified
  1 30 motor fuel pump station tax credit in an amount equal to five
  1 31 percent of a taxpayer's equity investment in a qualified motor
  1 32 fuel pump station.
  1 33    3.  Any tax credit in excess of the taxpayer's liability
  1 34 for the tax year is not refundable but may be credited to the
  1 35 tax liability for the following five years or until depleted,
  2  1 whichever is earlier.
  2  2    4.  In order for an equity investment to qualify for the tax
  2  3 credit, the business in which the equity investment is made
  2  4 shall, within one hundred twenty days of the first investment,
  2  5 do all of the following:
  2  6    a.  Submit an application to the department, in the manner
  2  7 and form prescribed by the department, to be verified as a
  2  8 qualified motor fuel pump station.
  2  9    b.  Notify the department of the names, addresses, equity
  2 10 issued, consideration paid for the equity interest, and the
  2 11 amount of any tax credits, of all equity owners who may qualify
  2 12 for the tax credits.  The list of equity owners who may qualify
  2 13 for the tax credits shall be amended as new equity investments
  2 14 are sold or as any information on the list changes.
  2 15    5.  After verifying the eligibility of a qualified motor fuel
  2 16 pump station, the department shall allow the claiming of tax
  2 17 credits as provided in subsection 2.
  2 18    Sec. 2.  EFFECTIVE UPON ENACTMENT.  This Act, being deemed of
  2 19 immediate importance, takes effect upon enactment.
  2 20    Sec. 3.  APPLICABILITY.  This Act applies to equity
  2 21 investments made on or after the effective date of this Act.
  2 22    Sec. 4.  RETROACTIVE APPLICABILITY.  This Act applies
  2 23 retroactively to January 1, 2015, for tax years beginning on
  2 24 or after that date.
  2 25                           EXPLANATION
  2 26 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  2 27 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  2 28    This bill provides an individual income tax credit for
  2 29 an individual who makes an equity investment in a qualified
  2 30 motor fuel pump station.  The tax credit is equal to 5 percent
  2 31 of the individual's equity investment.  "Equity investment",
  2 32 "qualified motor fuel pump station", and other related terms
  2 33 are defined in the bill.
  2 34    In order to qualify for the tax credit, a business must,
  2 35 within 120 days of the first investment, apply to the
  3  1 department of revenue to be verified as a qualified motor fuel
  3  2 pump station and must provide certain investor and investment
  3  3 information.
  3  4    The tax credit is nonrefundable, but any amount in excess of
  3  5 the tax liability may be carried forward for up to five years.
  3  6    The bill takes effect upon enactment and applies to
  3  7 equity investments made on or after that date, and applies
  3  8 retroactively to tax years beginning on or after January 1,
  3  9 2015.
