  3 19                           EXPLANATION
  3 20 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  3 21 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  3 22    This bill provides that a person who operates a dairy farm
  3 23 may sell milk or a milk product that is unpasteurized or
  3 24 ungraded, if it is produced at that dairy farm and sold to an
  3 25 individual as commercial feed for animals.  The person would
  3 26 not be required to obtain a permit or comply with inspection
  3 27 requirements as otherwise provided in the "Iowa Grade 'A' Milk
  3 28 Inspection Law" (Code chapter 192).  The person also would
  3 29 not be required to obtain a license or comply with inspection
  3 30 requirements under the "Iowa Commercial Feed Law" (Code chapter
  3 31 198).  The bill requires that a container holding unpasteurized
  3 32 or ungraded milk or a milk product be conspicuously labeled.  A
  3 33 person who purchases milk or a milk product for commercial feed
  3 34 for resale is guilty of a simple misdemeanor (new Code section
  3 35 192.113A).  A simple misdemeanor is punishable by confinement
  4  1 for no more than 30 days or a fine of at least $65 but not more
  4  2 than $625 or by both.
