  1 24                           EXPLANATION
  1 25 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 26 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 27    This bill relates to the sales and use tax exemption for
  1 28 sales of goods and services made in fulfillment of a written
  1 29 construction contract with a nonprofit hospital licensed
  1 30 pursuant to Code chapter 135B.  In order to qualify for the
  1 31 sales and use tax exemption under current law, the sales of
  1 32 goods or services must have occurred between July 1, 1998, and
  1 33 December 31, 2001, and the written construction contract or
  1 34 construction bonds must have been entered into or issued prior
  1 35 to December 31, 1999.
  2  1 The bill amends these dates to provide that the exemption
  2  2 will apply if the sales of goods or services occur between
  2  3 January 1, 2014, and December 31, 2016, and if the written
  2  4 construction contract is entered into on or after January 1,
  2  5 2014, but before December 31, 2015, or the construction bonds
  2  6 are issued on or after July 1, 2014, but before December 31,
  2  7 2015.
  2  8    Also under current law, the exemption only applies if the
  2  9 goods or services were purchased by a contractor as the agent
  2 10 for the hospital or were purchased directly by the hospital.
  2 11 The bill provides that the exemption will also apply if the
  2 12 goods or services were purchased directly by a contractor.
  2 13    By operation of Code section 423.6, an item exempt from the
  2 14 imposition of the sales tax is also exempt from the use tax
  2 15 imposed in Code section 423.5.
  2 16    The bill takes effect upon enactment and applies
  2 17 retroactively to January 1, 2014, to sales of goods and
  2 18 services on or after that date.

  1 26 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 27    This bill relates to the sales and use tax exemption for
  1 28 sales of goods and services made in fulfillment of a written
  1 29 construction contract with a nonprofit hospital licensed
  1 30 pursuant to Code chapter 135B.  In order to qualify for the
  1 31 sales and use tax exemption under current law, the sales of
  1 32 goods or services must have occurred between July 1, 1998, and
  1 33 December 31, 2001, and the written construction contract or
  1 34 construction bonds must have been entered into or issued prior
  1 35 to December 31, 1999.
  2  1 The bill amends these dates to provide that the exemption
  2  2 will apply if the sales of goods or services occur between
  2  3 January 1, 2014, and December 31, 2016, and if the written
  2  4 construction contract is entered into on or after January 1,
  2  5 2014, but before December 31, 2015, or the construction bonds
  2  6 are issued on or after July 1, 2014, but before December 31,
  2  7 2015.
  2  8    Also under current law, the exemption only applies if the
  2  9 goods or services were purchased by a contractor as the agent
  2 10 for the hospital or were purchased directly by the hospital.
  2 11 The bill provides that the exemption will also apply if the
  2 12 goods or services were purchased directly by a contractor.
  2 13    By operation of Code section 423.6, an item exempt from the
  2 14 imposition of the sales tax is also exempt from the use tax
  2 15 imposed in Code section 423.5.
  2 16    The bill takes effect upon enactment and applies
  2 17 retroactively to January 1, 2014, to sales of goods and
  2 18 services on or after that date.
