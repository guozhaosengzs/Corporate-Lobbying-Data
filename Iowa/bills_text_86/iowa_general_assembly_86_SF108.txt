  1  1    Section 1.    1  1    Section 1.    1  2 credit.
  1  3    1.  a.  An apprenticeship training tax credit shall be
  1  4 allowed against the taxes imposed in chapter 422, divisions II
  1  5 and III, for a portion of the taxpayer's costs in providing
  1  6 wages to apprentices in the construction trade trained under an
  1  7 apprenticeship program.
  1  8    b.  An individual may claim a tax credit under this
  1  9 subsection of a partnership, limited liability company,
  1 10 S corporation, estate, or trust electing to have income
  1 11 taxed directly to the individual.  The amount claimed by the
  1 12 individual shall be based upon the pro rata share of the
  1 13 individual's earnings from the partnership, limited liability
  1 14 company, S corporation, estate, or trust.
  1 15    c.  Any tax credit in excess of the taxpayer's liability
  1 16 for the tax year is not refundable. A tax credit shall not be
  1 17 carried back to a tax year prior to the tax year in which the
  1 18 taxpayer first receives the tax credit.
  1 19    2.  a.  To be eligible for the tax credit, the taxpayer
  1 20 shall be an apprenticeship sponsor conducting an apprenticeship
  1 21 program for an apprentice working in the construction trade and
  1 22 employed at an Iowa worksite.
  1 23    b.  The tax credit shall be two dollars per hour multiplied
  1 24 by the total number of hours worked during the tax year by an
  1 25 apprentice working for a taxpayer described in paragraph "a".
  1 26 The amount of tax credit in any year received by a taxpayer for
  1 27 each apprentice may not exceed two thousand dollars or fifty
  1 28 percent of the wages the apprentice earned, whichever is less.
  1 29    3.  a.  To claim an apprenticeship training tax credit under
  1 30 this section, a taxpayer must include one or more tax credit
  1 31 certificates issued by the authority with the taxpayer's tax
  1 32 return, verifying the taxpayer's eligibility for the credit.
  1 33 A tax credit certificate shall not be included with a return
  1 34 filed for a taxable year beginning prior to the tax year listed
  1 35 on the certificate.
  2  1    b.  The tax credit certificate, unless rescinded by the
  2  2 authority, shall be accepted by the department of revenue as
  2  3 payment for taxes imposed pursuant to chapter 422, divisions II
  2  4 and III, subject to any conditions or restrictions placed by
  2  5 the authority upon the face of the tax credit certificate and
  2  6 subject to the limitations of this section.
  2  7    4.  a.  The authority shall accept applications from eligible
  2  8 employers for an apprenticeship training tax credit.
  2  9    b.  If the authority determines the employer qualifies for
  2 10 a tax credit pursuant to this section, the authority shall
  2 11 issue an apprenticeship training tax credit certificate to
  2 12 be included with the taxpayer's tax return.  The tax credit
  2 13 certificate shall contain the taxpayer's name, address, tax
  2 14 identification number, the amount of the credit, the name of
  2 15 the qualifying employer, the name of each apprentice, and any
  2 16 other information required by the department of revenue.
  2 17    c.  Tax credit certificates issued under this section are not
  2 18 transferrable to any person or entity.
  2 19    5.  The authority shall adopt rules pursuant to chapter
  2 20 17A to administer this section, including rules governing
  2 21 the application process and the criteria used to evaluate
  2 22 applications.

  2 35                           EXPLANATION
  3  1 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  3  2 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  3  3    This bill creates a tax credit for apprenticeship sponsors
  3  4 employing an apprentice working in the construction trade
  3  5 at Iowa worksites.  The tax credit is available against the
  3  6 individual and corporate income tax for a portion of the wages
  3  7 paid to apprentices in the construction trade trained through
  3  8 an apprenticeship program.
  3  9    The amount of the credit is equal to $2 per hour multiplied
  3 10 by the total number of hours that an apprentice worked during
  3 11 the tax year.  The bill limits the amount of the tax credit
  3 12 in any year received by a taxpayer for each apprentice to a
  3 13 maximum of $2,000 or 50 percent of the wages the apprentice
  3 14 earned, whichever is less.
  3 15    The bill requires the taxpayer to be an apprenticeship
  3 16 sponsor conducting an apprenticeship program registered with
  3 17 the United States department of labor, office of apprenticeship
  3 18 through Iowa for an apprentice working in the construction
  3 19 trade and employed at an Iowa worksite.
  3 20    The tax credit is not refundable, and a tax credit shall not
  3 21 be carried back to a tax year prior to the tax year in which the
  3 22 taxpayer first receives the tax credit.  The tax credit is not
  3 23 transferable.
  3 24    To claim an apprenticeship training tax credit, a taxpayer
  3 25 must include with the tax return a tax credit certificate
  3 26 issued by the economic development authority.  The bill directs
  3 27 the economic development authority to adopt rules for the
  3 28 issuance of the tax credit certificates.
  3 29    The bill applies to tax years beginning on or after January
  3 30 1, 2016.
