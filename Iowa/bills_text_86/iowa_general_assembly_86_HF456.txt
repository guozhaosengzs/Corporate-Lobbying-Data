  1 34 any of the following:

  2  2    (2)  Treating the muscle tonus system for the purpose of
  2  3 enhancing health or muscle relaxation.
  2  4    (3)  Increasing range of motion, reducing stress, relieving
  2  5 pain, or improving circulation.
  2  6    b.  The use of animal massage includes but is not limited
  2  7 to myotherapy; massotherapy; therapeutic massage including
  2  8 hydrotherapy, and superficial hot or and cold applications;
  2  9 vibration or topical applications; or the use of a device that
  2 10 mimics the actions of the human hand when performing a function
  2 11 described in paragraph "a".
  2 12    3.  The use of applied animal behavior science must utilize a
  2 13 technique that evaluates or modifies animal behavior, assesses
  2 14 behavioral pathology, or provides socialization training.
  2 15    4.  The use of animal massage or applied animal behavior
  2 16 science does not include any of the following:
  2 17    a.  An accepted livestock management practice.
  2 18    b.  The diagnosis or treatment of a physical or mental
  2 19 disease, deformity, defect, wound, laceration, bone dislocation
  2 20 or fracture, or an injury to a cell, organ, or blood vessel.
  2 21    c.  An adjustment or manipulation of any articulation of the
  2 22 animal's spine.
  2 23    d.  Acupuncture involving the use of needles.
  2 24    e.  The administration or prescription of drugs.

  2 26 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  2 27 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  2 28    This bill provides that the use of animal massage and
  2 29 applied animal behavior science performed solely for purposes
  2 30 of treating a horse is not considered as part of the practice
  2 31 of veterinary medicine. Its use is not regulated by the Iowa
  2 32 board of veterinary medicine, and a person who performs animal
  2 33 massage or applied animal behavior science is not subject to
  2 34 the "Iowa Veterinary Practice Act" (Code chapter 169). The
  2 35 bill provides that animal massage must involve manipulating
  3  1 muscles or connective tissue; treating the muscle tonus system
  3  2 to enhance health or muscle relaxation; or increasing range
  3  3 of motion, reducing stress, relieving pain, or improving
  3  4 circulation.  The bill provides that the use of applied animal
  3  5 behavior science must utilize a technique that evaluates or
  3  6 modifies animal behavior, assesses behavioral pathology, or
  3  7 provides socialization training.
