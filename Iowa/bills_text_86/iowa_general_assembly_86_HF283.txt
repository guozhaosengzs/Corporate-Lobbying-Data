                                 HOUSE FILE                                  HOUSE FILE                                  BY  COMMITTEE ON EDUCATION

                                 (SUCCESSOR TO HF 126)

 A BILL FOR

  1 An Act relating to the time period over which payments are made
  2    under the all Iowa opportunity scholarship program.
  3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF IOWA:
    TLSB 1622HV (2) 86
    kh/sc

PAG LIN



  1  1    Section 1.  Section 261.87, subsection 3, Code 2015, is
  1  2 amended by striking the subsection and inserting in lieu
  1  3 thereof the following:
  1  4    3.  Extent of scholarship.  A qualified student at an
  1  5 eligible institution may receive scholarships for not more than
  1  6 the equivalent of two full=time academic years of undergraduate
  1  7 study, excluding summer semesters, or the equivalent.
  1  8 Scholarships awarded pursuant to this section shall be awarded
  1  9 on an annual basis and shall not exceed the lesser of the
  1 10 following, as determined by the commission:
  1 11    a.  The student's financial need.
  1 12    b.  One=half of the average resident tuition rate and
  1 13 mandatory fees established for institutions of higher learning
  1 14 governed by the state board of regents.
  1 15    c.  The resident tuition and mandatory fees charged for the
  1 16 program of enrollment by the eligible institution at which the
  1 17 student is enrolled.
  1 18                           EXPLANATION
  1 19 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 20 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 21    This bill authorizes a qualified student at an eligible
  1 22 institution, which is defined in the Code as a community
  1 23 college or regents university, to receive an all Iowa
  1 24 opportunity scholarship for not more than two full=time
  1 25 academic years of undergraduate study, excluding summer
  1 26 semesters. The scholarship must be awarded by the college
  1 27 student aid commission to the student on an annual basis.
  1 28 Under current law, a qualified student who receives the
  1 29 scholarship must each year meet the eligibility requirements
  1 30 established in the Code.

  1 21    This bill authorizes a qualified student at an eligible
  1 22 institution, which is defined in the Code as a community
  1 23 college or regents university, to receive an all Iowa
  1 24 opportunity scholarship for not more than two full=time
  1 25 academic years of undergraduate study, excluding summer
  1 26 semesters. The scholarship must be awarded by the college
  1 27 student aid commission to the student on an annual basis.
  1 28 Under current law, a qualified student who receives the
  1 29 scholarship must each year meet the eligibility requirements
  1 30 established in the Code.
