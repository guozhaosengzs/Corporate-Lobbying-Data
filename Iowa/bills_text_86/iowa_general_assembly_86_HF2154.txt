  1 12 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 13 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 14    This bill makes changes to the penalties for littering.
  1 15 "Littering" is defined in Code section 455B.361 as any garbage,
  1 16 rubbish, trash, refuse, waste materials, or debris.
  1 17    The bill changes the criminal penalty for a person
  1 18 convicted of littering from a simple misdemeanor to a serious
  1 19 misdemeanor. A simple misdemeanor is punishable by confinement
  1 20 for no more than 30 days or a fine of at least $65 but not more
  1 21 than $625 or by both. A serious misdemeanor is punishable by
  1 22 confinement for no more than one year and a fine of at least
  1 23 $315 but not more than $1,875.
  1 24    The bill also requires the court, in imposing a sentence
  1 25 upon a person convicted of littering, to order the person to
  1 26 reimburse the director of the department of natural resources
  1 27 for the costs of cleaning up the litter.
