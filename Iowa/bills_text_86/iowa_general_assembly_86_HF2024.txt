  1 19 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 20 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 21    This bill relates to the expungement of the criminal record
  1 22 for a simple misdemeanor interference with official acts.
  1 23    The bill provides that upon the expiration of two years
  1 24 following a conviction for a violation of simple misdemeanor
  1 25 interference with official acts, or of a similar local
  1 26 ordinance, a person may petition the court to expunge the
  1 27 conviction, and if the person has had no other criminal
  1 28 convictions, other than local traffic violations or simple
  1 29 misdemeanor violations of Code chapter 321 during the two=year
  1 30 period, the conviction shall be expunged as a matter of law.
  1 31 The bill further provides that the court shall enter an order
  1 32 that the record of the conviction be expunged by the clerk of
  1 33 the district court.
  1 34    Upon receipt of from the clerk of the district court that
  1 35 a record of conviction has been expunged for a violation of
  2  1 simple misdemeanor interference with official acts, the bill
  2  2 requires that the record of conviction shall be removed from
  2  3 the criminal history data files maintained by the department
  2  4 of public safety.
  2  5    A simple misdemeanor interference with official acts is
  2  6 punishable by confinement for no more than 30 days or a fine of
  2  7 at least $250 but not more than $625 or by both.

  1 21    This bill relates to the expungement of the criminal record
  1 22 for a simple misdemeanor interference with official acts.
  1 23    The bill provides that upon the expiration of two years
  1 24 following a conviction for a violation of simple misdemeanor
  1 25 interference with official acts, or of a similar local
  1 26 ordinance, a person may petition the court to expunge the
  1 27 conviction, and if the person has had no other criminal
  1 28 convictions, other than local traffic violations or simple
  1 29 misdemeanor violations of Code chapter 321 during the two=year
  1 30 period, the conviction shall be expunged as a matter of law.
  1 31 The bill further provides that the court shall enter an order
  1 32 that the record of the conviction be expunged by the clerk of
  1 33 the district court.
  1 34    Upon receipt of from the clerk of the district court that
  1 35 a record of conviction has been expunged for a violation of
  2  1 simple misdemeanor interference with official acts, the bill
  2  2 requires that the record of conviction shall be removed from
  2  3 the criminal history data files maintained by the department
  2  4 of public safety.
  2  5    A simple misdemeanor interference with official acts is
  2  6 punishable by confinement for no more than 30 days or a fine of
  2  7 at least $250 but not more than $625 or by both.
