  1  3    1.  A person shall not place a blind or stand for hunting
  1  4 on public land unless the blind or stand is affixed with and
  1  5 exhibits a metal plate which plainly shows the name and address
  1  6 of the owner of the blind or stand. For a tree blind or stand,
  1  7 the metal plate shall be affixed on the front of the foot
  1  8 platform of the blind or stand on the third step up from the
  1  9 ground. For a pop=up blind or stand, the metal plate shall be
  1 10 affixed to or hung from the zipper head or access hook of the
  1 11 pop=up blind or stand.
  1 12    2.  For purposes of this section, "blind or stand" means
  1 13 a device or an enclosure used for concealment while hunting,
  1 14 constructed either wholly or partially from man=made materials,
  1 15 and used by a person who is hunting for the purpose of hiding
  1 16 from sight.  A "blind or stand" is not a naturally occurring
  1 17 landscape feature or an arrangement of natural or agricultural
  1 18 plant material that a hunter uses for concealment.  The natural
  1 19 resource commission shall adopt rules pursuant to chapter 17A
  1 20 to implement this section.

  1 27 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 28 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 29    This bill requires that a blind or stand placed on public
  1 30 land for hunting be affixed with and exhibit a metal plate
  1 31 which plainly shows the name and address of the owner of the
  1 32 blind or stand.
  1 33    For a tree blind or stand, the metal plate must be affixed
  1 34 on the front of the foot platform of the blind or stand on the
  1 35 third step up from the ground.  For a pop=up blind or stand, the
  2  1 metal plate must be affixed to or hung from the zipper head or
  2  2 access hook of the blind or stand.
  2  3    For purposes of the bill, a "blind or stand" is a device or
  2  4 an enclosure used for concealment while hunting, constructed
  2  5 either wholly or partially from man=made materials, and used by
  2  6 a person who is hunting for the purpose of hiding from sight.
  2  7 A "blind or stand" does not include a naturally occurring
  2  8 landscape feature or an arrangement of natural or agricultural
  2  9 plant material that a hunter uses for concealment. The natural
  2 10 resource commission is required to adopt rules to implement the
  2 11 provisions of the bill.  A violation of the provisions of the
  2 12 bill is punishable by a scheduled fine of $20.
