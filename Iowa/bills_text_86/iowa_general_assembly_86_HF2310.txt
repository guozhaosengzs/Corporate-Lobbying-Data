  3 26                           EXPLANATION
  3 27 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  3 28 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  3 29    This bill amends current law that requires smoke detectors
  3 30 in multiple=unit residential buildings and single=family
  3 31 dwellings to also require the installation of carbon monoxide
  3 32 alarms, as defined in the bill, in such buildings.
  3 33    The bill requires the installation of carbon monoxide alarms
  3 34 in multiple=unit residential buildings and single=family
  3 35 dwellings constructed on or after July 1, 2017, and that have
  4  1 a fuel=fired heater or appliance as defined in the bill, a
  4  2 fireplace, or an attached garage. Carbon monoxide alarms
  4  3 shall be installed within 15 feet of the entrance of each
  4  4 room lawfully used for sleeping purposes or in a location as
  4  5 specified by rule. In addition, the state fire marshal shall
  4  6 adopt rules for the installation of carbon monoxide alarms
  4  7 in the same manner in existing multiple=unit residential
  4  8 buildings and single=family rental units that have a fuel=fired
  4  9 appliance, a fireplace, or an attached garage. The owner of
  4 10 a single=family dwelling that has a fuel=fired appliance, a
  4 11 fireplace, or an attached garage is responsible for installing
  4 12 carbon monoxide alarms in the same manner and shall certify
  4 13 such installation upon filing for a homestead credit. Owners
  4 14 of multiple=unit residential buildings and single=family rental
  4 15 units that have a fuel=fired appliance, a fireplace, or an
  4 16 attached garage are also required to supply light=emitting
  4 17 carbon monoxide alarms for hearing=impaired tenants.
  4 18    Current requirements applicable to smoke alarms are also
  4 19 made applicable to carbon monoxide alarms in the bill. The
  4 20 bill provides that the state fire marshal shall enforce the
  4 21 requirements of the bill concerning carbon monoxide alarms
  4 22 and provides that an occupant of a multiple=unit residential
  4 23 building or single=family rental unit in which the owner fails
  4 24 to install or fix a carbon monoxide alarm within 14 days
  4 25 of receiving written notice may deduct the cost of fixing
  4 26 or installing a carbon monoxide alarm from the next rental
  4 27 payment. In addition, a person is prohibited from making
  4 28 a carbon monoxide alarm inoperable. A person who violates
  4 29 a provision of the bill concerning carbon monoxide alarms
  4 30 is guilty of a simple misdemeanor. A simple misdemeanor is
  4 31 punishable by confinement for no more than 30 days or a fine of
  4 32 at least $65 but not more than $625, or by both.
  4 33    The bill takes effect July 1, 2017.
