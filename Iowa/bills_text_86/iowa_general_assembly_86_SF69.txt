  3  4    This bill relates to the filling of school district board
  3  5 vacancies.
  3  6    The bill maintains current law for the filling of a vacancy
  3  7 that occurs not later than 45 days before a regular school
  3  8 election. The bill also maintains current law requiring that
  3  9 the school board fill all other vacancies occurring among the
  3 10 officers and members of the school board by appointment.
  3 11    The bill requires that the board publish notice of the intent
  3 12 of the board to fill a vacancy among the members of a school
  3 13 board by appointment, but that the electors of the school
  3 14 district have the right to file a petition requiring that the
  3 15 vacancy be filled by a special election.  The bill allows the
  3 16 board to publish the notice in advance of a vacancy if the
  3 17 officer or member of the board submits a resignation that will
  3 18 take effect at a future date. The bill allows the board to
  3 19 make an appointment after the notice is published or after the
  3 20 vacancy occurs, whichever is later.  The bill provides that the
  3 21 appointment is considered temporary if a valid petition to call
  3 22 a special election is filed with the secretary of the school
  3 23 board within 14 days after publication of the notice or within
  3 24 14 days after the appointment is made.
  3 25    The bill provides that a valid petition must be signed by
  3 26 at least 100 eligible electors or eligible electors equaling
  3 27 at least 30 percent of the number of voters at the preceding
  3 28 regular school election, whichever is greater.
  3 29    The bill requires that a special election be called by the
  3 30 school board upon the filing of a valid petition. The special
  3 31 election shall be held in accordance with requirements under
  3 32 current law for filling school district board vacancies.  If
  3 33 the board is reduced below a quorum, the secretary of the
  3 34 board, or if there is no secretary, the area education agency
