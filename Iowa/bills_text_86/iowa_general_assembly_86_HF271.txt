  1 35                           EXPLANATION
  2  1 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  2  2 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  2  3    This bill modifies the required core curriculum and
  2  4 twenty=first century learning skills and knowledge that the
  2  5 state board of education is required to address in rule.
  2  6    Currently, the required core curriculum includes English or
  2  7 language arts, mathematics, science, and social studies; and
  2  8 the necessary twenty=first century learning skills include but
  2  9 are not limited to civic literacy, health literacy, technology
  2 10 literacy, financial literacy, and employability skills.  The
  2 11 bill requires that such core curriculum and twenty=first
  2 12 century learning skills include music, visual art, drama and
  2 13 theater, and other fine and applied arts.  The bill also
  2 14 requires the department of education to employ a consultant
  2 15 to oversee the development of and compliance with the fine
  2 16 arts core curriculum and to provide guidance for professional
  2 17 development programs, strategies, and materials based on
  2 18 the rules relating to the fine arts core curriculum and the
  2 19 twenty=first century fine arts learning skills adopted by the
  2 20 state board.
  2 21    The bill may include a state mandate as defined in Code
  2 22 section 25B.3.  The bill requires that the state cost of
  2 23 any state mandate included in the bill be paid by a school
  2 24 district from state school foundation aid received by the
  2 25 school district under Code section 257.16.  The specification
  2 26 is deemed to constitute state compliance with any state mandate
  2 27 funding=related requirements of Code section 25B.2.  The
  2 28 inclusion of this specification is intended to reinstate the
  2 29 requirement of political subdivisions to comply with any state
  2 30 mandates included in the bill.

  2  2 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  2  3    This bill modifies the required core curriculum and
  2  4 twenty=first century learning skills and knowledge that the
  2  5 state board of education is required to address in rule.
  2  6    Currently, the required core curriculum includes English or
  2  7 language arts, mathematics, science, and social studies; and
  2  8 the necessary twenty=first century learning skills include but
  2  9 are not limited to civic literacy, health literacy, technology
  2 10 literacy, financial literacy, and employability skills.  The
  2 11 bill requires that such core curriculum and twenty=first
  2 12 century learning skills include music, visual art, drama and
  2 13 theater, and other fine and applied arts.  The bill also
  2 14 requires the department of education to employ a consultant
  2 15 to oversee the development of and compliance with the fine
  2 16 arts core curriculum and to provide guidance for professional
  2 17 development programs, strategies, and materials based on
  2 18 the rules relating to the fine arts core curriculum and the
  2 19 twenty=first century fine arts learning skills adopted by the
  2 20 state board.
  2 21    The bill may include a state mandate as defined in Code
  2 22 section 25B.3.  The bill requires that the state cost of
  2 23 any state mandate included in the bill be paid by a school
  2 24 district from state school foundation aid received by the
  2 25 school district under Code section 257.16.  The specification
  2 26 is deemed to constitute state compliance with any state mandate
  2 27 funding=related requirements of Code section 25B.2.  The
  2 28 inclusion of this specification is intended to reinstate the
  2 29 requirement of political subdivisions to comply with any state
  2 30 mandates included in the bill.
