  1 18                           EXPLANATION
  1 19 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 20 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 21    This bill relates to statewide assessments approved by
  1 22 the state board of education and administered to students by
  1 23 Iowa's school districts and requires that the state board of
  1 24 education's rules specify that the Iowa assessments developed
  1 25 by Iowa testing programs are the approved district=wide
  1 26 assessments of student progress administered for purposes of
  1 27 the core academic indicators. Iowa testing programs is a
  1 28 research, development, and outreach unit in the college of
  1 29 education at the university of Iowa, which has been responsible
  1 30 for creating and administering the Iowa test of basic skills
  1 31 and the Iowa test of educational development to Iowa's students
  1 32 for several decades.  In recent years, Iowa testing programs
  1 33 modified the tests and renamed them the Iowa assessments.  The
  1 34 Iowa assessments have been used by school districts statewide
  1 35 since the 2011=2012 academic year, as directed in the Code.
  2  1 The department uses the results from the Iowa assessments for
  2  2 federal annual yearly progress and annual progress reporting
  2  3 purposes.
  2  4    The bill prohibits the state board and the department of
  2  5 education from adopting, administering, or approving the
  2  6 administration of assessments developed by the smarter balanced
  2  7 assessment consortium. Initiated by the council of chief state
  2  8 school officers and the national governors association center
  2  9 for best practices, the smarter balanced assessment consortium
  2 10 is a state=led consortium working collaboratively to develop
  2 11 assessments aligned to the common core state standards that
  2 12 accurately measure student progress toward college and career
  2 13 readiness.
  2 14    The bill strikes a Code provision that directs the state
  2 15 board's rules to require that all students enrolled in school
  2 16 districts in grades 3 through 11 be administered an assessment
  2 17 during the last quarter of the school year that at a minimum
  2 18 assesses the core academic indicators identified in the Code;
  2 19 is aligned with the Iowa common core standards in both content
  2 20 and rigor; accurately describes student achievement and growth
  2 21 for purposes of the school, the school district, and state
  2 22 accountability systems; and provides valid, reliable, and
  2 23 fair measures of student progress toward college or career
  2 24 readiness.
  2 25    The bill also strikes a Code provision that provides for
  2 26 the establishment of an assessment task force to review and
  2 27 make recommendations for a statewide assessment of student
  2 28 progress on the core academic indicators, and a Code provision
  2 29 that directs the state board to submit to the general assembly
  2 30 recommendations for modifications of assessments of student
  2 31 progress.  The task force and the state board submitted their
  2 32 recommendations during the 2014=2015 fiscal year.
