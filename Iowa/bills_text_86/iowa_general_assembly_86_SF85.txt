  1  1    Section 1.    1  1    Section 1.    1  2 ==== training course.
  1  3    1.  The academy shall develop training standards for the use
  1  4 of electronic control devices, including stun guns and tasers,
  1  5 by law enforcement agencies throughout the state.
  1  6    2.  The training standards shall include the completion of an
  1  7 electronic control devices safety course by peace officers and
  1  8 jailers prior to the use of electronic control devices.  The
  1  9 electronic control devices safety course shall include but is
  1 10 not limited to the following:
  1 11    a.  Developing a basic knowledge and understanding of
  1 12 electronic control devices that encompasses their use, storage,
  1 13 and maintenance.
  1 14    b.  Diagnosing malfunctions.
  1 15    c.  Identifying conditions and circumstances where the use of
  1 16 electronic control devices is appropriate or inappropriate.
  1 17    d.  Identifying persons whose health may be severely impacted
  1 18 by the use of electronic control devices.
  1 19    3.  The training standards shall also require law
  1 20 enforcement agencies to do the following:
  1 21    a.  Conduct an annual review relating to agency use of
  1 22 electronic control devices.
  1 23    b.  Retrain peace officers and jailers as new electronic
  1 24 control devices are purchased or as new techniques are
  1 25 developed.
  1 26    c.  Develop and adopt agency policies relating to the use of
  1 27 electronic control devices.
  1 28    4.  The academy shall adopt rules to administer this section.
  1 29    Sec. 2.  IMPLEMENTATION OF ACT.  Section 25B.2, subsection
  1 30 3, shall not apply to this Act.
  1 31                           EXPLANATION
  1 32 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 33 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 34    This bill requires the Iowa law enforcement academy to
  1 35 develop training standards for the use of electronic control
  2  1 devices, including stun guns and tasers, by law enforcement
  2  2 agencies throughout the state.
  2  3    Under the bill, the training standards shall include the
  2  4 completion of an electronic control devices safety course by
  2  5 peace officers and jailers prior to the use of such devices.
  2  6 The electronic control devices safety course shall include the
  2  7 following: developing a basic knowledge and understanding
  2  8 of control devices that encompasses their use, storage, and
  2  9 maintenance; diagnosing malfunctions; identifying conditions
  2 10 and circumstances where the use of devices is appropriate or
  2 11 inappropriate; and identifying persons whose health may be
  2 12 severely impacted by the use of devices.
  2 13    The training standards under the bill shall also require law
  2 14 enforcement agencies to do the following: conduct an annual
  2 15 review relating to agency use of electronic control devices,
  2 16 retrain peace officers and jailers when new electronic control
  2 17 devices are purchased or as new techniques are developed, and
  2 18 adopt agency policies relating to the use of electronic control
  2 19 devices.
  2 20    The bill may include a state mandate as defined in Code
  2 21 section 25B.3.  The bill makes inapplicable Code section 25B.2,
  2 22 subsection 3, which would relieve a political subdivision from
  2 23 complying with a state mandate if funding for the cost of
  2 24 the state mandate is not provided or specified.  Therefore,
  2 25 political subdivisions are required to comply with any state
  2 26 mandate included in the bill.

  2  1 devices, including stun guns and tasers, by law enforcement

  2  4 completion of an electronic control devices safety course by
  2  5 peace officers and jailers prior to the use of such devices.
  2  6 The electronic control devices safety course shall include the
  2  7 following: developing a basic knowledge and understanding
  2  8 of control devices that encompasses their use, storage, and
  2  9 maintenance; diagnosing malfunctions; identifying conditions
  2 10 and circumstances where the use of devices is appropriate or
  2 11 inappropriate; and identifying persons whose health may be
  2 12 severely impacted by the use of devices.

  2 14 enforcement agencies to do the following: conduct an annual
  2 15 review relating to agency use of electronic control devices,
  2 16 retrain peace officers and jailers when new electronic control
  2 17 devices are purchased or as new techniques are developed, and
  2 18 adopt agency policies relating to the use of electronic control
  2 19 devices.

  2 21 section 25B.3.  The bill makes inapplicable Code section 25B.2,
  2 22 subsection 3, which would relieve a political subdivision from
  2 23 complying with a state mandate if funding for the cost of
  2 24 the state mandate is not provided or specified.  Therefore,
  2 25 political subdivisions are required to comply with any state
  2 26 mandate included in the bill.
