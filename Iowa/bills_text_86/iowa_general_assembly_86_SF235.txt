  2 10 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  2 11 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  2 12    This bill relates to the regulation of pharmacy benefits
  2 13 managers.
  2 14    The bill provides that after notice and hearing, the
  2 15 commissioner of insurance may impose any or all of the
  2 16 sanctions set out in Code section 507B.7 and may suspend
  2 17 or revoke a pharmacy benefits manager's certificate of
  2 18 registration as a third=party administrator, upon finding that
  2 19 the pharmacy benefits manager violated any of the requirements
  2 20 of Code chapter 510B (regulation of pharmacy benefits
  2 21 managers), 510 (regulation of third=party administrators), or
  2 22 507B (regulation of insurance trade practices).
  2 23    The bill provides that a pharmacy benefits manager is
  2 24 subject to the commissioner's authority to conduct an
  2 25 examination pursuant to Code chapter 507B; conduct an
  2 26 investigation pursuant to Code chapter 507B; and conduct an
  2 27 examination, audit, or inspection pursuant to Code chapter 510
  2 28 for third=party administrators.  The bill also provides that
  2 29 when the commissioner undertakes any of these activities, all
  2 30 notes, work papers, or other documents related to the activity
  2 31 are confidential records under Code chapter 22 and are accorded
  2 32 the same confidentiality as such materials related to the
  2 33 examination of an insurer as provided in Code chapter 507.
  2 34    The bill is effective upon enactment.
