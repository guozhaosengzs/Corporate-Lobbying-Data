  3 11    This bill requires that certain persons regulated by the
  3 12 department of agriculture and land stewardship are required
  3 13 to comply with requirements for moving dogs or cats from a
  3 14 location outside this state to a location inside this state.
  3 15 These persons include an animal rescue or animal foster care
  3 16 recognized by the department, or a  commercial establishment
  3 17 operating as an animal shelter, dealer, pet store, or pound
  3 18 operating pursuant to an authorization issued by the department
  3 19 such as a certificate of registration, state license, or
  3 20 permit.  The bill prohibits a person from moving a dog or
  3 21 cat under four months old into this state for purposes of
  3 22 sale.  It requires all of the following: (1) a dog or cat
  3 23 be kept in a secure location to determine if it is afflicted
  3 24 with an infectious or contagious disease or parasites prior
  3 25 to moving in this state, (2) a dog has reacted negatively to
  3 26 a brucellosis test, (3) a dog at least four months of age be
  3 27 administered a current rabies vaccination, and (4) a dog or
  3 28 cat be accompanied by a certificate of veterinary inspection.
  3 29 The bill's restrictions do not apply to persons who exercised
  3 30 custody and control of a dog or cat in this state prior to its
  3 31 movement out of state so long as the person did not relinquish
  3 32 control or custody of the dog or cat during its movement.  The
  3 33 bill provides that the department may take disciplinary action
  3 34 against an animal shelter, dealer, pet store, or pound by
  3 35 suspending or revoking its authorization to operate in this
  4  1 state.  An animal rescue, animal foster care, animal shelter,
  4  2 dealer, pet store, or pound that fails to comply with the
  4  3 bill's provisions is also subject to a civil penalty of at
  4  4 least $1,000 and not more than $5,000.
