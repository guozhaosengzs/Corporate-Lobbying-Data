  1  5    An electric utility required to be rate=regulated under
  1  6 this chapter shall produce or purchase, by January 1 of the
  1  7 designated year, the following minimum amounts of electricity
  1  8 generated by solar energy facilities:
  1  9    1.  Fifty megawatts in 2017.
  1 10    2.  One hundred megawatts in 2018.
  1 11    3.  One hundred seventy=five megawatts in 2019.
  1 12    4.  Two hundred fifty megawatts in 2020.
  1 13    5.  Three hundred fifty megawatts in 2021.
  1 14    6.  Four hundred fifty megawatts in 2022.
  1 15    7.  Five hundred seventy=five megawatts in 2023.
  1 16    8.  Seven hundred megawatts in 2024.
  1 17    9.  Eight hundred fifty megawatts in 2025.
  1 18    10.  One thousand megawatts in 2026, and each year
  1 19 thereafter.
  1 20                           EXPLANATION
  1 21 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 22 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 23    Electric utilities are currently required to either own or
  1 24 purchase their proportionate share of 105 megawatts of power
  1 25 from alternative energy production facilities or small hydro
  1 26 facilities, as those terms are defined in Code section 476.42.
  1 27 That proportionate share is allocated by the Iowa utilities
  1 28 board.
  1 29    This bill replaces this requirement with a solar energy
  1 30 minimum purchase standard applicable to electric utilities
  1 31 required to be rate=regulated under Code chapter 476. The bill
  1 32 requires rate=regulated electric utilities to either produce or
  1 33 purchase a specified amount of solar energy, to be phased=in
  1 34 over a 10=year period.  The 10=year phase=in commences with
  1 35 a 50 megawatt minimum purchase requirement by January 1,
  2  1 2017, and concludes with a 1,000 megawatt minimum purchase
  2  2 requirement by January 1, 2026, and each year thereafter.
