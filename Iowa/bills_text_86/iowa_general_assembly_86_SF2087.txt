  1 21 second degree in violation of section 711.3 shall be counted
  1 22 as a previous conviction in determining whether a person is a

  3 10 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with

  3 12    This bill establishes a criminal penalty for a violent
  3 13 repeat offender and reduces earned time for offenders
  3 14 required to participate in batterers' education under certain
  3 15 circumstances.
  3 16    Under the bill, a violent repeat offender is any person
  3 17 convicted of a more serious felony under Code chapter
  3 18 707 (homicide), 708 (assault), 709 (sexual abuse), 710
  3 19 (kidnapping), 711 (robbery and extortion), or 713 (burglary)
  3 20 who has twice before been convicted of any felony violation
  3 21 under those Code chapters.
  3 22    An offender who is serving a violent repeat offender
  3 23 sentence under the bill is only eligible for a reduction of
  3 24 sentence equal to fifteen eighty=fifths of a day for each
  3 25 day of good conduct by the offender. Most other offenders
  3 26 are eligible for a reduction of sentence equal to one and
  3 27 two=tenths days for each day the inmate demonstrates good
  3 28 conduct.  The fifteen eighty=fifths of a day for each day
  3 29 of good conduct by a repeat violent offender equals the same
  3 30 rate of reduction of sentence for an offender who is serving
  3 31 a 70 percent sentence under Code section 902.12. However, an
  3 32 offender serving a violent repeat offender sentence is not
  3 33 required to serve seven=tenths of the maximum term of the
  3 34 sentence prior to being eligible for parole or work release as
  3 35 an offender serving a 70 percent sentence is required to serve.
  4  1 In order to preserve the service of a 70 percent sentence by
  4  2 an offender, a person shall not be a violent repeat offender
  4  3 if the most recent conviction that would otherwise make the
  4  4 offender a violent repeat offender is a conviction for murder
  4  5 in the second degree in violation of Code section 707.3,
  4  6 attempted murder in violation of Code section 707.11, sexual
  4  7 abuse in the second degree in violation of Code section 709.3,
  4  8 kidnapping in the second degree in violation of Code section
  4  9 710.3, robbery in the first degree in violation of Code section
  4 10 711.2, or robbery in the second degree in violation of Code
  4 11 section 711.3. However, a prior conviction for murder in the
  4 12 second degree, attempted murder, sexual abuse in the second
  4 13 degree, kidnapping in the second degree, robbery in the first
  4 14 degree, or robbery in the second degree shall be counted as
  4 15 a previous conviction in determining whether a person is a
  4 16 violent repeat offender.
  4 17    The bill provides that an inmate at a correctional
  4 18 institution who is required to participate in a batterers'
  4 19 education program is not eligible for a reduction of sentence
