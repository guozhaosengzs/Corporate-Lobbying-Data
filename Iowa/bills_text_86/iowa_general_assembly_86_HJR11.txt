 HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION

  1 A Joint Resolution proposing an amendment to the Constitution
  2    of the State of Iowa relating to an individual's right to
  3    acquire, keep, possess, transport, carry, transfer, and use
  4    arms.
  5 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF THE STATE OF IOWA:
    TLSB 1098YH (4) 86
    jm/rj

PAG LIN



  1  1    Section 1.  The following amendment to the Constitution of
  1  2 the State of Iowa is proposed:
  1  3    Article I of the Constitution of the State of Iowa is amended
  1  4 by adding the following new section:
  1  5 Right to acquire, keep, possess, transport, carry, transfer,
  1  6 and use arms.SEC. 1A.  The right of the people to keep and
  1  7 bear arms, as herein expressed, shall not be infringed.
  1  8    The right of an individual to acquire, keep, possess,
  1  9 transport, carry, transfer, and use arms to defend life and
  1 10 liberty and for all other legitimate purposes is fundamental
  1 11 and shall not be infringed upon or denied.  Mandatory
  1 12 licensing, registration, or special taxation as a  condition
  1 13 of the exercise of this right is prohibited, and any other
  1 14 restriction shall be subject to strict scrutiny.
  1 15    Sec. 2.  REFERRAL AND PUBLICATION.  The foregoing proposed
  1 16 amendment to the Constitution of the State of Iowa is referred
  1 17 to the general assembly to be chosen at the next general
  1 18 election for members of the general assembly and the secretary
  1 19 of state is directed to cause the same to be published for
  1 20 three consecutive months previous to the date of that election
  1 21 as provided by law.
  1 22                           EXPLANATION
  1 23 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 24 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 25    This joint resolution proposes an amendment to the
  1 26 Constitution of the State of Iowa.
  1 27    The joint resolution expresses that the right of an
  1 28 individual to acquire, keep, possess, transport, carry,
  1 29 transfer, and use arms to defend life and liberty and for all
  1 30 other legitimate purposes is fundamental and shall not be
  1 31 infringed or denied.  The joint resolution also provides that
  1 32 mandatory licensing, registration, or special taxation as a
  1 33 condition of the exercise of this right is prohibited and any
  1 34 other restriction shall be subject to strict scrutiny.
  1 35    The joint resolution, if adopted, would be referred to the
  2  1 next general assembly for adoption a second time before being
  2  2 submitted to the electorate for ratification.
