  1 30                           EXPLANATION
  1 31 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 32 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 33    This bill relates to the criminal offense of harassment.
  1 34    The bill modifies the criminal offense of harassment in
  1 35 violation of Code section 708.7 to include when a person, with
  2  1 intent to intimidate, annoy, or alarm another person, films the
  2  2 genitals, anus, or female breast of the other person, or shows
  2  3 the other person engaged in a sex act, without the consent of
  2  4 the other person. A person who violates this provision commits
  2  5 harassment in the first degree. Harassment in the first degree
  2  6 is an aggravated misdemeanor. An aggravated misdemeanor is
  2  7 punishable by confinement for no more than two years and a fine
  2  8 of at least $625 but not more than $6,250.
  2  9    The bill modifies the criminal offense of harassment to also
  2 10 include when a person, with intent to intimidate, annoy, or
  2 11 alarm another person, publishes or distributes an electronic or
  2 12 printed photograph, picture, or film that shows the genitals,
  2 13 anus, or female breast of the other person, or shows the
  2 14 other person engaged in a sex act, without the consent of the
  2 15 other person. A person who violates this provision commits
  2 16 harassment in the second degree. Harassment in the second
  2 17 degree is a serious misdemeanor. A serious misdemeanor is
  2 18 punishable by confinement for no more than one year and a fine
  2 19 of at least $315 but not more than $1,875.
  2 20    A person who commits any type of harassment in violation of
  2 21 Code section 708.7 also commits interference with judicial acts
  2 22 if the harassment involves a judicial officer, court employee,
  2 23 or family member of a judicial officer or court employee,
  2 24 and the harassment is with the intent to interfere with or
  2 25 improperly influence, or in retaliation for, the official
  2 26 acts of a judicial officer or court employee.  A person who
  2 27 commits interference with judicial acts commits an aggravated
  2 28 misdemeanor.
  2 29    A person convicted of harassment may also be required to
  2 30 register as a sex offender if the offense is determined to be
  2 31 sexually motivated.
