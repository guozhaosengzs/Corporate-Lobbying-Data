  1 35                           EXPLANATION
  2  1 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  2  2 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  2  3    BILL == SUMMARY.  This bill provides that a seed exchange
  2  4 or seed library is exempt from certain requirements otherwise
  2  5 imposed on persons engaged in selling, distributing,
  2  6 transporting, offering for sale, or advertising agricultural
  2  7 seed.  The bill provides that any agricultural seed owned and
  2  8 possessed by a seed exchange or seed library is exempt from
  2  9 inspection, analysis, and testing requirements (Code sections
  2 10 199.10 and 199.11) or paying a fee for obtaining a permit (Code
  2 11 section 199.15).
  2 12    BILL == SEED EXCHANGE AND SEED LIBRARY DEFINED.  The bill
  2 13 defines a "seed exchange" as an individual, a government
  2 14 entity, a person organized and operating on a nonprofit
  2 15 or cooperative basis, or another entity entirely owned by
  2 16 individuals (e.g., as shareholders of a corporation), if the
  2 17 person is engaged in transferring agricultural seed to persons
  2 18 in return for agricultural seed of equivalent value, up to
  2 19 $100,000 during any 12=month period.  The bill defines a "seed
  2 20 library" as a government entity, or a person organized and
  2 21 operating on a cooperative or nonprofit basis, that donates
  2 22 and receives donations of agricultural seed for promotion,
  2 23 education, or transfer without receiving legal consideration
  2 24 in return.
  2 25    BACKGROUND.  The department of agriculture and land
  2 26 stewardship acting with Iowa state university of science and
  2 27 technology provides for the regulation of agricultural seed
  2 28 sold, offered for sale, exposed for sale, or transported
  2 29 within this state.  Agricultural seed includes grass, forage,
  2 30 cereal, oil, fiber, and any other kind of crop seed, lawn seed,
  2 31 vegetable seed, or seed mixtures.

  2  3    BILL == SUMMARY.  This bill provides that a seed exchange
  2  4 or seed library is exempt from certain requirements otherwise
  2  5 imposed on persons engaged in selling, distributing,
  2  6 transporting, offering for sale, or advertising agricultural
  2  7 seed.  The bill provides that any agricultural seed owned and
  2  8 possessed by a seed exchange or seed library is exempt from
  2  9 inspection, analysis, and testing requirements (Code sections
  2 10 199.10 and 199.11) or paying a fee for obtaining a permit (Code
  2 11 section 199.15).
  2 12    BILL == SEED EXCHANGE AND SEED LIBRARY DEFINED.  The bill
  2 13 defines a "seed exchange" as an individual, a government
  2 14 entity, a person organized and operating on a nonprofit
  2 15 or cooperative basis, or another entity entirely owned by
  2 16 individuals (e.g., as shareholders of a corporation), if the
  2 17 person is engaged in transferring agricultural seed to persons
  2 18 in return for agricultural seed of equivalent value, up to
  2 19 $100,000 during any 12=month period.  The bill defines a "seed
  2 20 library" as a government entity, or a person organized and
  2 21 operating on a cooperative or nonprofit basis, that donates
  2 22 and receives donations of agricultural seed for promotion,
  2 23 education, or transfer without receiving legal consideration
  2 24 in return.

  2 29 within this state.  Agricultural seed includes grass, forage,
  2 30 cereal, oil, fiber, and any other kind of crop seed, lawn seed,
  2 31 vegetable seed, or seed mixtures.
