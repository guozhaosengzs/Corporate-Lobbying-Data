  4 15    This bill is related to the vehicle registration duties of
  4 16 county treasurers.
  4 17    The bill provides that a county treasurer may collect
  4 18 delinquent parking fines from a vehicle registration applicant
  4 19 on behalf of the county or certain cities. The bill requires
  4 20 the applicant to remit full payment for the delinquent parking
  4 21 fines, along with a $5 processing fee and the renewal fee,
  4 22 to the county treasurer at the time of registration renewal.
  4 23 A county treasurer collecting on behalf of the county or a
  4 24 city is required to update the vehicle registration records
  4 25 through the state department of transportation's distributed
  4 26 teleprocessing network, on a daily basis, for all persons who
  4 27 have paid delinquent parking fines in this manner. The bill
  4 28 also makes corresponding changes.
  4 29    The bill strikes language related to payment of computer
  4 30 programming costs incurred by the department of transportation
  4 31 to accommodate the collection of delinquent parking fines on
  4 32 behalf of cities through Code chapter 28E agreements between
  4 33 counties and cities and replaces that payment with a $5 fee
  4 34 that the county treasurer may collect for the collection of
  4 35 such fines.
  5  1 The bill further provides that a county treasurer may
  5  2 cooperate with the clerk of the district court to collect
  5  3 delinquent court debt owed to the clerk.  The bill provides
  5  4 that a county treasurer may lift a hold on vehicle registration
  5  5 if an applicant remits full payment for the applicant's
  5  6 delinquent court debt. The county treasurer may also collect a
  5  7 $5 fee for the collection of such court debt for deposit in the
  5  8 county general fund.
