  2  7 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  2  8 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  2  9    This bill relates to reading instruction and dyslexia
  2 10 interventions for public school students, and practitioner
  2 11 preparation in reading.
  2 12    Under the bill, each practitioner preparation program
  2 13 approved by the state board of education must include at least
  2 14 three semester credit hours of coursework relating to reading
  2 15 disabilities, including dyslexia.
  2 16      For purposes of Code section 279.68, the bill strikes the
  2 17 current definition of "dyslexia" and provides that "dyslexia"
  2 18 means a specific learning disability that is neurobiological
  2 19 in origin and is characterized by difficulties with accurate
  2 20 and fluent word recognition and by poor spelling and decoding
  2 21 abilities. "Dyslexia" includes difficulties that typically
  2 22 result from a deficit in the phonological component of language
  2 23 that is often unexpected in relation to other cognitive
  2 24 abilities and other effective classroom instruction, and may
  2 25 include secondary consequences such as problems in reading
  2 26 comprehension and reduced reading experience that can impede
  2 27 growth of vocabulary and background knowledge.
  2 28    INTERVENTIONS, SERVICES, AND SUPPORTS.  The bill requires
  2 29 that all intensive interventions, instructional services, and
  2 30 supports provided under Code section 279.68 to kindergarten
  2 31 through grade three students with reading deficiencies shall
  2 32 be systematic, structured, multisensory, direct, explicit, and
  2 33 sequential.
