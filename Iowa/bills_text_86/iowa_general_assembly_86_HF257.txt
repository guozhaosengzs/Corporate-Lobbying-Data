  3 20                           EXPLANATION
  3 21 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  3 22 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  3 23    This bill modifies the criteria for claiming the deduction
  3 24 from the individual income tax, corporate income tax, and
  3 25 franchise tax of certain wages paid relating to the employment
  3 26 of an individual with a disability.
  3 27    Under current law, a business that has 20 or fewer full=time
  3 28 equivalent employees, or that has not more than $3 million
  3 29 in annual gross revenues, may claim a deduction equal to 65
  3 30 percent of the wages paid to an individual with a disability
  3 31 that was hired during the year.  The bill amends these
  3 32 eligibility criteria to provide that a business that has 500
  3 33 or fewer full=time equivalent employees, or that has not more
  3 34 than $21 million in annual gross revenues, may qualify for the
  3 35 deduction.  The bill changes the name of a business meeting the
  4  1 criteria for the deduction from "small business" to "eligible
  4  2 business".
  4  3    Also under current law, the deduction excludes a business
  4  4 that is an affiliate or subsidiary of a business dominant in
  4  5 its field of operation.  The bill amends the definition of
  4  6 "dominant in its field of operation" from a business having
  4  7 more than 20 full=time equivalent employees and more than $3
  4  8 million in annual gross revenues, to a business having more
  4  9 than 500 full=time equivalent employees and more than $21
  4 10 million in annual gross revenues.
  4 11    The bill takes effect upon enactment and applies to
  4 12 individuals with a disability hired on or after that date.  The
  4 13 bill applies retroactively to January 1, 2015, for tax years
  4 14 beginning on or after that date.
