  1 16 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 17 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 18    Under current law, in order to qualify as a community
  1 19 college instructor in the subject area of career and technical
  1 20 education, a person must have a baccalaureate or graduate
  1 21 degree in the area or a related area of study or occupational
  1 22 area in which the community college instructor is teaching
  1 23 classes or must have special training and at least 6,000 hours
  1 24 of recent and relevant work experience in the occupational area
  1 25 or related area. This bill allows a licensed practitioner who
  1 26 holds a career and technical endorsement on the practitioner's
  1 27 Iowa practitioner license and who has work experience in a
  1 28 career and technical occupational area as a classroom teacher
  1 29 in a school district or accredited nonpublic school to count
  1 30 those hours of teaching toward the 6,000 hours of relevant work
  1 31 experience.
