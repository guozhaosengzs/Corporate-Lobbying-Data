  1 17                           EXPLANATION
  1 18 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 19 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 20    This bill relates to consent decrees in juvenile delinquency
  1 21 proceedings.
  1 22    Under current law, after a county attorney files a petition
  1 23 alleging that a child has committed a delinquent act and prior
  1 24 to an order of adjudication, the court may suspend the juvenile
  1 25 delinquency proceedings, enter a consent decree, and continue
  1 26 the case under terms and conditions established by the court.
  1 27 If the child complies with the terms and conditions of the
  1 28 consent decree for the required amount of time, the original
  1 29 petition may not be reinstated. If the child fails to comply
  1 30 with the terms and conditions of the consent decree, the child
  1 31 may be held accountable for the underlying delinquent act as if
  1 32 the consent decree had never been entered. However, the court
  1 33 may not enter a consent decree unless the child and the child's
  1 34 parent, guardian, or custodian are informed of the consequences
  1 35 of the consent decree and the child agrees to the terms and
  2  1 conditions of the consent decree.
  2  2    The bill amends current law so that the court may enter a
  2  3 consent decree only after the child admits to having committed
  2  4 a delinquent act, the court finds that the child committed a
  2  5 delinquent act, or the child and the county attorney agree to
  2  6 the consent decree.
