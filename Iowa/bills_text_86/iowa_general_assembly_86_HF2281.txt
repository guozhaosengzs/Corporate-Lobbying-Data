  1 29                           EXPLANATION
  1 30 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 31 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 32    This bill relates to the possession of a pistol, revolver, or
  1 33 ammunition by persons under the age of 14.
  1 34    Current law prohibits all persons including a parent,
  1 35 guardian, or spouse from allowing a person under 14 years of
  2  1 age to possess a pistol, revolver, or the ammunition therefor.
  2  2 A person who violates current law commits a serious misdemeanor
  2  3 for a first offense and a class "D" felony for any second or
  2  4 subsequent offense.
  2  5    Current law does allow a person at least 14 years of age but
  2  6 under 21 years of age to possess a pistol, revolver, or the
  2  7 ammunition therefor, if such person has consent from a parent,
  2  8 guardian, or spouse who is 21 years of age or older. Current
  2  9 law also permits a person under 21 years of age but at least
  2 10 18 years of age, while on military duty, or while acting as
  2 11 a peace officer, security guard, or correctional officer, to
  2 12 possess a pistol, revolver, or the ammunition therefor without
  2 13 consent from a parent, guardian, or spouse.
  2 14    The bill allows a parent, guardian, or spouse who is 21 years
  2 15 of age or older, while under the direct supervision of the
  2 16 parent, guardian, or spouse, or an instructor 21 years of age
  2 17 or older with the consent of the parent, guardian, or spouse,
  2 18 to permit a person under 14 years of age to possess a pistol or
  2 19 revolver or the ammunition therefor, which may then be lawfully
  2 20 used.
  2 21    The bill provides that a parent or guardian or spouse who
  2 22 is 21 years of age or older, of a minor under 14 years of age,
  2 23 who allows that minor to possess a pistol or revolver or the
  2 24 ammunition therefor for any lawful purpose while under the
  2 25 direct supervision of the parent or guardian or spouse who
  2 26 is 21 years of age or older, shall be strictly liable to an
  2 27 injured party for all damages resulting from the possession of
  2 28 the pistol or revolver or ammunition therefor by that minor.
  2 29    The bill defines "direct supervision" for purposes of Code
  2 30 section 724.22 to mean supervision provided by another person
  2 31 who maintains visual and verbal contact at all times with the
  2 32 supervised person.
  2 33    The bill takes effect upon enactment.

  1 31 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 32    This bill relates to the possession of a pistol, revolver, or
  1 33 ammunition by persons under the age of 14.
  1 34    Current law prohibits all persons including a parent,
  1 35 guardian, or spouse from allowing a person under 14 years of
  2  1 age to possess a pistol, revolver, or the ammunition therefor.
  2  2 A person who violates current law commits a serious misdemeanor
  2  3 for a first offense and a class "D" felony for any second or
  2  4 subsequent offense.
  2  5    Current law does allow a person at least 14 years of age but
  2  6 under 21 years of age to possess a pistol, revolver, or the
  2  7 ammunition therefor, if such person has consent from a parent,
  2  8 guardian, or spouse who is 21 years of age or older. Current
  2  9 law also permits a person under 21 years of age but at least
  2 10 18 years of age, while on military duty, or while acting as
  2 11 a peace officer, security guard, or correctional officer, to
  2 12 possess a pistol, revolver, or the ammunition therefor without
  2 13 consent from a parent, guardian, or spouse.
  2 14    The bill allows a parent, guardian, or spouse who is 21 years
  2 15 of age or older, while under the direct supervision of the
  2 16 parent, guardian, or spouse, or an instructor 21 years of age
  2 17 or older with the consent of the parent, guardian, or spouse,
  2 18 to permit a person under 14 years of age to possess a pistol or
  2 19 revolver or the ammunition therefor, which may then be lawfully
  2 20 used.
  2 21    The bill provides that a parent or guardian or spouse who
  2 22 is 21 years of age or older, of a minor under 14 years of age,
  2 23 who allows that minor to possess a pistol or revolver or the
  2 24 ammunition therefor for any lawful purpose while under the
  2 25 direct supervision of the parent or guardian or spouse who
  2 26 is 21 years of age or older, shall be strictly liable to an
  2 27 injured party for all damages resulting from the possession of
  2 28 the pistol or revolver or ammunition therefor by that minor.
  2 29    The bill defines "direct supervision" for purposes of Code
  2 30 section 724.22 to mean supervision provided by another person
  2 31 who maintains visual and verbal contact at all times with the
  2 32 supervised person.
  2 33    The bill takes effect upon enactment.
