  1 18 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with

  1 20    This bill exempts from the sales tax the sales price of
  1 21 chemicals, sorbents, or reagents sold, or test laboratory
  1 22 services furnished, to a nonprofit human blood collection
  1 23 and processing establishment that meets the definition of
  1 24 establishment in 21 C.F.R. {607.3(c). That federal regulation
  1 25 defines "establishment" to mean a place of business under
  1 26 one management at one general physical location.  The term
  1 27 includes, among others, human blood and plasma donor centers,
  1 28 blood banks, transfusion services, other blood product

  1 19 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 20    This bill exempts from the sales tax the sales price of
  1 21 chemicals, sorbents, or reagents sold, or test laboratory
  1 22 services furnished, to a nonprofit human blood collection
  1 23 and processing establishment that meets the definition of
  1 24 establishment in 21 C.F.R. {607.3(c). That federal regulation
  1 25 defines "establishment" to mean a place of business under
  1 26 one management at one general physical location.  The term
  1 27 includes, among others, human blood and plasma donor centers,
  1 28 blood banks, transfusion services, other blood product
  1 29 manufacturers, and independent laboratories that engage in
  1 30 quality control and testing for registered blood product
  1 31 establishments.
  1 32    By operation of Code section 423.6, an item exempt from the
  1 33 imposition of the sales tax is also exempt from the use tax
  1 34 imposed in Code section 423.5.
  1 35    The bill prohibits refunds of taxes, interest, or penalties
  2  1 arising from claims resulting from the enactment of the bill
  2  2 for sales occurring between July 1, 2005, and the effective
  2  3 date of the bill.
  2  4    The bill takes effect upon enactment and applies
  2  5 retroactively to July 1, 2005.
