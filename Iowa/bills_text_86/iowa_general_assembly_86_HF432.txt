  1  5    a.  In order to provide additional funds for school districts
  1  6 with district transportation costs per pupil in excess of
  1  7 the state average transportation costs per pupil, as those
  1  8 amounts are determined under section 257.31, subsection 17,
  1  9 a supplementary weighting plan for determining enrollment is
  1 10 adopted.
  1 11    b.  A supplementary weighting amount per pupil as determined
  1 12 under paragraph "c" shall be assigned to each transported pupil
  1 13 of a school district that meets the requirement of paragraph
  1 14 "a".
  1 15    c.  For each budget year, the department of management shall
  1 16 calculate a supplementary weighting amount per pupil using data
  1 17 from the base year to generate an amount for each transported
  1 18 pupil in each school district meeting the requirement of
  1 19 paragraph "a" equal to fifteen million dollars divided by the
  1 20 total number of transported pupils in all school districts that
  1 21 meet the requirement of paragraph "a".
  1 22    d.  Eligibility for supplementary weighting under this
  1 23 subsection shall not affect a school district's eligibility for
  1 24 transportation assistance under section 257.31, subsection 17.
  1 25    Sec. 2.  EFFECTIVE UPON ENACTMENT.  This Act, being deemed of
  1 26 immediate importance, takes effect upon enactment.
  1 27    Sec. 3.  APPLICABILITY.  This Act applies to school budget
  1 28 years beginning on or after July 1, 2015.
  1 29                           EXPLANATION
  1 30 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 31 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 32    This bill establishes a supplementary weighting plan for
  1 33 those school districts with district transportation costs per
  1 34 pupil in excess of the state average transportation costs
  1 35 per pupil.  The bill requires the department of management
  2  1 to calculate a supplementary weighting amount per pupil for
  2  2 each school district meeting such a requirement to generate
  2  3 an amount for each transported pupil in each eligible school
  2  4 district equal to $15 million divided by the total number of
  2  5 transported pupils in all eligible school districts.
  2  6    The bill provides that eligibility for supplementary
  2  7 weighting under the bill does not affect a school district's
  2  8 eligibility for transportation assistance from the school
  2  9 budget review committee under Code section 257.31(17).
  2 10    The bill takes effect upon enactment and applies to school
  2 11 budget years beginning on or after July 1, 2015.
