  1  1    Section 1.    1  1    Section 1.    1  2 tax credit.
  1  3    1.  For purposes of this section, unless the context
  1  4 otherwise requires:
  1  5    a.  "Apprentice" means an Iowa resident who is at least
  1  6 sixteen years of age, except where a higher minimum age is
  1  7 required by law, who is employed in an occupation approved for
  1  8 apprenticeship by the United States department of labor, office
  1  9 of apprenticeship, and is registered with the United States
  1 10 department of labor, office of apprenticeship.
  1 11    b.  "Apprenticeship program" means a program registered
  1 12 with the United States department of labor, office of
  1 13 apprenticeship, which includes terms and conditions for the
  1 14 qualification, recruitment, selection, employment, and training
  1 15 of apprentices, including the requirement for a written
  1 16 apprenticeship agreement.
  1 17    2.  a.  An apprenticeship training tax credit shall be
  1 18 allowed against the taxes imposed in this division and division
  1 19 III, for a portion of the taxpayer's costs, as described
  1 20 in subsection 3, in providing wages to apprentices in the
  1 21 construction trade training under an apprenticeship program.
  1 22    b.  An individual may claim a tax credit under this
  1 23 subsection of a partnership, limited liability company,
  1 24 S corporation, estate, or trust electing to have income
  1 25 taxed directly to the individual.  The amount claimed by the
  1 26 individual shall be based upon the pro rata share of the
  1 27 individual's earnings from the partnership, limited liability
  1 28 company, S corporation, estate, or trust.
  1 29    c.  Any credit in excess of the taxpayer's liability for the
  1 30 tax year is not refundable and shall not be credited to the tax
  1 31 liability for any following year or carried back to a tax year
  1 32 prior to the tax year in which the taxpayer claims the credit.
  1 33    d.  A tax credit issued pursuant to this section is not
  1 34 transferable to any other taxpayer.
  1 35    3.  a.  To be eligible for the tax credit, the taxpayer shall
  2  1 be the employer of an apprentice working in the construction
  2  2 trade and employed at an Iowa worksite.
  2  3    b.  The tax credit shall be two dollars per hour multiplied
  2  4 by the total number of hours worked during the tax year by an
  2  5 apprentice working for a taxpayer described in paragraph "a".
  2  6 The amount of tax credit in any year received by a taxpayer for
  2  7 each apprentice may not exceed two thousand dollars or fifty
  2  8 percent of the wages the apprentice earned, whichever is less.
  2  9    4.  To claim an apprenticeship training tax credit under
  2 10 this section, a taxpayer must include verification of the
  2 11 taxpayer's eligibility for the credit, including evidence
  2 12 of the apprentice's residence, on a form prescribed by the
  2 13 department and included with the taxpayer's tax return.
  2 14    5.  The director of revenue shall adopt rules pursuant to
  2 15 chapter 17A to administer this section.

  2 24                           EXPLANATION
  2 25 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  2 26 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  2 27    This bill creates an apprenticeship training tax credit
  2 28 available against the individual and corporate income tax for
  2 29 wages paid by employers to apprentices in the construction
  2 30 trade who are training under an apprenticeship program at an
  2 31 Iowa worksite.
  2 32    The amount of the credit is equal to $2 per hour multiplied
  2 33 by the total number of hours that an apprentice worked during
  2 34 the tax year.  The bill limits the amount of the tax credit
  2 35 in any year received by a taxpayer for each apprentice to a
  3  1 maximum of $2,000 or 50 percent of the wages the apprentice
  3  2 earned, whichever is less.
  3  3    The bill requires the taxpayer to be the employer of an
  3  4 apprentice working in the construction trade and employed at an
  3  5 Iowa worksite.  The bill requires the apprentice to be an Iowa
  3  6 resident, registered with the United States department of labor
  3  7 office of apprenticeship, working in the construction trade,
  3  8 and training under an apprenticeship program.
  3  9    The tax credit is not refundable, and a tax credit shall not
  3 10 be carried forward or carried back to a tax year prior to the
  3 11 tax year in which the taxpayer first receives the tax credit.
  3 12 The tax credit is not transferable.
  3 13    To claim an apprenticeship training tax credit, the taxpayer
  3 14 must include verification of the taxpayer's eligibility for the
  3 15 credit, including evidence of the apprentice's residence, with
  3 16 the taxpayer's tax return.
  3 17    The bill applies to tax years beginning on or after January
  3 18 1, 2016, for wages paid on or after that date to apprentices
  3 19 hired on or after July 1, 2015.

  2 23 after that date to apprentices hired on or after July 1, 2015.
  2 24                           EXPLANATION
  2 25 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  2 26 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  2 27    This bill creates an apprenticeship training tax credit
  2 28 available against the individual and corporate income tax for
  2 29 wages paid by employers to apprentices in the construction
  2 30 trade who are training under an apprenticeship program at an
  2 31 Iowa worksite.
  2 32    The amount of the credit is equal to $2 per hour multiplied
  2 33 by the total number of hours that an apprentice worked during
  2 34 the tax year.  The bill limits the amount of the tax credit
  2 35 in any year received by a taxpayer for each apprentice to a
  3  1 maximum of $2,000 or 50 percent of the wages the apprentice
  3  2 earned, whichever is less.
  3  3    The bill requires the taxpayer to be the employer of an
  3  4 apprentice working in the construction trade and employed at an
  3  5 Iowa worksite.  The bill requires the apprentice to be an Iowa
  3  6 resident, registered with the United States department of labor
  3  7 office of apprenticeship, working in the construction trade,
  3  8 and training under an apprenticeship program.
  3  9    The tax credit is not refundable, and a tax credit shall not
  3 10 be carried forward or carried back to a tax year prior to the
  3 11 tax year in which the taxpayer first receives the tax credit.
  3 12 The tax credit is not transferable.
  3 13    To claim an apprenticeship training tax credit, the taxpayer
  3 14 must include verification of the taxpayer's eligibility for the
  3 15 credit, including evidence of the apprentice's residence, with
  3 16 the taxpayer's tax return.
  3 17    The bill applies to tax years beginning on or after January
  3 18 1, 2016, for wages paid on or after that date to apprentices
  3 19 hired on or after July 1, 2015.
