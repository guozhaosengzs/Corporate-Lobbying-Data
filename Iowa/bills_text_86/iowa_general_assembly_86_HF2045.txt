  1 21 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 22 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 23    This bill modifies the threshold damage dollar amounts which
  1 24 constitute the crimes of criminal mischief in the second,
  1 25 third, and fourth degrees. "Criminal mischief" means any
  1 26 damage, defacing, alteration, or destruction of property when
  1 27 done intentionally by one who has no right to so act.
  1 28    The bill changes the threshold dollar amounts to qualify for
  1 29 prosecution for criminal mischief in the second degree from
  1 30 damage exceeding $1,000 but not exceeding $10,000 to damage
  1 31 exceeding $2,500 but not exceeding $10,000. Criminal mischief
  1 32 in the second degree remains a class "D" felony. A class "D"
  1 33 felony is punishable by confinement for no more than five years
  1 34 and a fine of at least $750 but not more than $7,500.
  1 35    The bill changes the threshold dollar amounts to qualify
  2  1 for prosecution for criminal mischief in the third degree
  2  2 from damage exceeding $500 but not exceeding $1,000 to damage
  2  3 exceeding $1,000 but not exceeding $2,500.  Criminal mischief
  2  4 in the third degree remains an aggravated misdemeanor. An
  2  5 aggravated misdemeanor is punishable by confinement for no more
  2  6 than two years and a fine of at least $625 but not more than
  2  7 $6,250.
  2  8    The bill changes the threshold dollar amounts to qualify
  2  9 for prosecution for criminal mischief in the fourth degree
  2 10 from damage exceeding $200 but not exceeding $500 to damage
  2 11 exceeding $200 but not exceeding $1,000. Criminal mischief in
  2 12 the fourth degree remains a serious misdemeanor.  A serious
  2 13 misdemeanor is punishable by confinement for no more than one
  2 14 year and a fine of at least $315 but not more than $1,875.
  2 15    The bill does not modify the crimes of criminal mischief in
  2 16 the first or fifth degree.
  2 17    By changing the threshold dollar amounts to qualify for
  2 18 prosecution for criminal mischief in the second, third, or
  2 19 fourth degree, the bill also changes the criminal offenses
  2 20 related to crops or crop operation property damage under Code
  2 21 section 717A.3.
