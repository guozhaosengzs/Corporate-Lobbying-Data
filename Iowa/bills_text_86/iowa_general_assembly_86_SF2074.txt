  1 21 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 22 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 23    If a person operates a motor vehicle with a denied, canceled,
  1 24 suspended, or revoked driver's license or without financial
  1 25 liability coverage in effect for the vehicle and an accident or
  1 26 collision occurs, this bill prohibits the person from bringing
  1 27 a civil action for property damage, personal injury, or death
  1 28 resulting from the accident or collision, regardless of fault.
  1 29    In addition, the bill provides that knowingly operating a
  1 30 motor vehicle with a denied, canceled, suspended, or revoked
  1 31 driver's license or without financial liability coverage in
  1 32 effect for the vehicle, while a child under 14 years of age
  1 33 or a minor under 18 years of age with a mental or physical
  1 34 disability is a passenger in the vehicle, constitutes child
  1 35 endangerment. Child endangerment is punishable as a class "B"
  2  1 felony if the child or minor dies, a class "C" felony if the
  2  2 child or minor is seriously injured, a class "D" felony if the
  2  3 child or minor is injured, and an aggravated misdemeanor if the
  2  4 child or minor is not injured.
