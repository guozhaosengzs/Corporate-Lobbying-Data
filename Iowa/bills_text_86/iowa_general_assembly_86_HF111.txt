  1 15 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with

  1 17    Generally, a motor vehicle is required to display two
  1 18 registration plates, with one on the front of the vehicle
  1 19 and one on the rear of the vehicle. Current law allows
  1 20 motor vehicles which are model year 1948 or older, or are
  1 21 reconstructed or specially constructed to resemble a model year
  1 22 1948 vehicle or older, to display only one registration plate
  1 23 on the rear of the vehicle if the other registration plate
  1 24 issued to the vehicle is carried in the vehicle at all times
  1 25 when the vehicle is operated on a public highway.
  1 26    This bill broadens that exception to include any motor
  1 27 vehicle originally manufactured without a bracket, device, or
  1 28 other means to display and secure a registration plate at the
  1 29 front of the vehicle.
  1 30    The scheduled fine for incorrect display of registration
  1 31 plates in violation of Code section 321.37 is $20.
