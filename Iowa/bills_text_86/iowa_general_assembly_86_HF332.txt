  1 13                           EXPLANATION
  1 14 The inclusion of this explanation does not constitute agreement with
  1 15 the explanation's substance by the members of the general assembly.
  1 16    This bill relates to the time period within which a
  1 17 prosecution for kidnapping in the first degree must be
  1 18 commenced.
  1 19    Under the bill, a prosecution for kidnapping in the first
  1 20 degree in violation of Code section 710.2 may be commenced at
  1 21 any time after the commission of the offense.  Current law
  1 22 requires that kidnapping in the first degree be prosecuted
  1 23 within three years of the commission of the offense.
  1 24    A person who commits kidnapping in the first degree commits
  1 25 a class "A" felony.  A class "A" felony is punishable by
  1 26 confinement for life without possibility of parole.  If a
  1 27 person is released prior to the expiration of the kidnapping in
  1 28 the first degree sentence, and the offense was determined to be
  1 29 sexually motivated, the person shall register as a sex offender
  1 30 pursuant to Code chapter 692A.
