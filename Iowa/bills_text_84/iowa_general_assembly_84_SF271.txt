IOWA:


Section

Section

317.11
Weeds
on
roads
——
harvesting
of
grass.


1.
The
county
boards
of
supervisors
and
the
state


department

transportation
shall
control
noxious
weeds
growing
on
the


roads
under
their

Spraying
for
control

noxious
weeds
shall
be
limited
to
those
circumstances
when
it


is
not
practical
to
mow
or
otherwise
control
the
noxious
weeds.


2.
a.


Nothing
under
this
chapter
shall
prevent
the


landowner
from
harvesting,
in
proper
season
on
or
after


July
15,
the
grass
grown
on
the
road
along


portion
of
the


right-of-way
beyond
the
shoulder
of
a
noninterstate
highway


that
is
adjacent
to
the
landowner’s
land
except
for
vegetation


maintained
for
highway
purposes
as
part
of
an
integrated


roadside
vegetation
management
plan
which
is
consistent
with


the
objectives
in
section
314.22
.


b.
With
approval
of
the
department
of
transportation
or
the


county
board
of
supervisors
of
the
county
having
jurisdiction


over
the
highway,
a
landowner
may

the
grass
growing


within
the
median
of
a
noninterstate
highway
that
runs
adjacent


to
the
landowner’s
land.
